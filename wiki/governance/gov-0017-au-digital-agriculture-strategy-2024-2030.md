# African Union Digital Agriculture Strategy governance and implementation boundary

## Status, instrument type and scope

The AU Digital Agriculture Strategy (DAS) is presented as a continental
strategy and implementation plan for 2024–2030. The package contains no AU
Assembly/STC adoption decision, national instrument, sanctions/enforcement
clause or appropriation record and is not by itself proof of legal force or
adoption by all 55 AU Member States. The PDF is dated October 2023; the
official AU record is dated 2024-02-12 and remained live when checked on
2026-07-12. A 2025 FAO/AUC record documents an implementation-support session
and uneven digital-agriculture progress across Africa, not measured DAS KPI
progress.

> **Claim record — GOV-0017-C01 | source-report**
> **Claim:** AU DAS is currently presented by the African Union as its
> 2024–2030 continental digital-agriculture strategy and implementation plan,
> while the dated companion documents an implementation-support session and
> uneven continental digital-agriculture progress rather than a DAS adoption,
> programme-completion or outcome census.
> **Claim status:** active
> **Scope:** Official AU publication state and one FAO/AUC implementation-
> support-context record checked 2026-07-12; not a legal-opinion or Member-State
> compliance survey.
> **As of:** 2026-07-12.
> **Review after:** 2026-10-10.
> **Evidence status:** single-source
> **Confidence:** high
> **Evidence:** [SRC-0032](../sources/src-0032-au-digital-agriculture-strategy-2024-2030.md),
> PDF cover and Executive Summary, printed pp. 4–6 (physical pp. 10–12);
> current AU HTML dated 2024-02-12; FAO/AUC HTML dated 2025-06-18.
> **Basis / limits:** Modality, dates and status language are direct. The
> companion is coordinated implementation-support context, not independent
> conformance or effectiveness evidence.

## Actors, levels and role/action allocation

The strategy assigns continental functions to AU/AUC and AUDA-NEPAD, regional
functions to RECs and national functions to Member States. It asks agriculture
and ICT/infrastructure authorities to coordinate and names trade, foreign
affairs, livestock, fisheries, environment, education and other sectors as
relevant. Private sector, civil society, researchers, extension/advisory
services, producers and development partners have enabling, implementation or
participation roles.

The role labels `C`, `R` and `N` in the activity matrix distinguish
continental, regional and national action. They do not establish a judicial,
inspection or enforcement hierarchy, RACI, accountable owner or assurance
chain; listed partners do not by themselves create accountability.

## Force, adoption and national implementation

The document recommends that Member States develop national digital/
e-agriculture strategies aligned with national development plans and integrate
existing initiatives. It calls for national legislative, policy and regulatory
work and for international frameworks, including the Malabo Convention on
Cyber Security, to be ratified/integrated where relevant. These are proposed
actions and targets, not evidence that a treaty is ratified, a national rule
exists or a target has been achieved.

## Data, cyber, traceability and resilience actions

The strategy proposes:

- interoperable, quality-controlled and shareable agricultural data platforms;
- validation and comprehensive metadata for collection, processing, date,
  ownership and provenance;
- continental minimum privacy and cybersecurity standards where national
  standards are unavailable;
- national policy/regulatory work and cross-sector task forces;
- farmer/animal identity, registries, traceability and certification;
- climate and pest/disease early-warning platforms;
- planned monitoring/evaluation, digital literacy, resilient infrastructure and
  financing/partnership support.

These measures span data governance, cyber policy, traceability,
agricultural pest/disease/climate early warning and resilience, but early
warning is not cyber detection. No complete cyber incident-response/recovery
standard, adoption result, test or independent effectiveness evidence is
provided.

## Implementation evidence and internal conflicts

The activity matrix assigns prospective outcomes, KPIs, dates and resource
estimates. Figure 9 presents preparation/mobilization, rollout and
consolidation/scaling horizons. The theory of change explicitly depends on
assumptions such as political will, funding, safe/secure infrastructure and
partner support.

The 42-activity A–G matrix has at least seven blank KPI cells, two literal
`X number` placeholders, output rather than outcome measures, unexplained
country/budget bases and large unestimated scopes. It gives no baseline,
measurement method, data owner, cadence, audit/assurance, corrective-action
rule, RACI or independent evaluation. At least six 2025-dated milestone types—
solar-irrigation and insurance studies, cyber/privacy minimum standards, a free-
data guidebook, an e-advisory study and a climate-data platform—have no
completion evidence in the 2025 companion or the AU publication page captured
in 2026.

The artifact also has unresolved quality defects: the 145/146 page conflict,
broken references, seven objectives versus a later `six pillars` label,
three incompatible budget presentations, malformed/duplicated country
clusters and 2000–2021 input indices with map/footnote/unit defects. Therefore
this page preserves targets as targets and makes no canonical budget,
appropriation, completion, current-readiness or KPI-achievement claim.

## One Health and multi-jurisdiction reconciliation boundary

AU DAS adds African agriculture/ICT/data/cyber and continental/REC/Member-State
predicates for later comparison with U.S. and global One Health sources. It
does not use One Health as its organizing framework, ingest current national
animal/plant/environmental law or prove adoption in a second legal
jurisdiction. It may inform a later `AGE-GR` reconciliation, but the source
transaction changes no frozen cell and exact lineage independence remains a
separately reviewed synthesis question.
