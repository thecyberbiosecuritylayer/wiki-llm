## Authority and applicability

SP 800-82 is a federal technical guideline, not a statute, regulation or proof
of sector adoption. Appendix F explains federal-context use and voluntary
availability for nongovernmental organizations. A specific obligation can
arise only from a separate authority, contract or incorporation not established
by this document alone.

> **Claim record — GOV-0008-C02 | source-report**
> **Claim:** Rev. 3 provides contextual federal OT guidance and voluntary
> nongovernmental use; it does not create one universal legal duty or evidence
> that an organization adopted its controls.

## Tailoring and compensating-control governance

The OT overlay is a partial starting point and expects further system/sector
tailoring. A compensating control must have documented rationale and comparable
protection, with residual risk addressed through the appropriate decision
authority. Calling something compensating is not a waiver or effectiveness
finding.

> **Claim record — GOV-0008-C03 | source-report**
> **Claim:** NIST describes context-specific tailoring and documented
> equivalence/residual-risk treatment for compensating controls rather than
> checklist adoption or unsupported waiver.
> **Claim status:** active
> **Scope:** Appendix F governance method; not an authorization or assessed
> compensating control.
> **As of:** 2023-09-28.
> **Review after:** 2027-01-08.
> **Evidence status:** single-source
> **Confidence:** high
> **Evidence:** [SRC-0018](../sources/src-0018-nist-sp-800-82r3-ot-security-2023.md),

> §F.4, printed pp. 223–224 (PDF pp. 240–241).
> **Basis / limits:** Tailoring/equivalence requirements are direct. No
> organization-specific decision, evidence or residual-risk acceptance exists.
