## Modality and legal boundary

NCI says adoption is voluntary and recommendations may be tailored to mission,
resource and study design. The guide points readers to federal/state/local law,
privacy/human-subject rules and other standards, but those citations do not
turn the NCI guidance itself into law or prove applicability/compliance.

> **Claim record — GOV-0012-C02 | source-report**
> **Claim:** The NCI Best Practices are official voluntary adaptable guidance,
> not a statute, regulation, mandatory accreditation standard, compliance
> finding or proof that any biospecimen resource implemented them.
> **Claim status:** active
> **Scope:** Instrument force and adoption evidence state; not legal advice or
> a determination about separately cited laws/conditions.
> **As of:** 2026-01.

## Scope and class boundary

The instrument covers human specimens and associated data for research,
physical storage structure and associated processes/policies, from formal
biobanks/biorepositories to small investigator-held collections. Clinical
source/testing discussion does not change its core research-resource scope;
post-mortem recovery is separately guided and high-containment/non-human scope
is not complete here.

> **Claim record — GOV-0012-C03 | source-report**
> **Claim:** The governance object is the human research biospecimen resource—
> material, associated data, physical structure and policies/processes—with
> explicit voluntary applicability and separate-scope limits.
> **Claim status:** active
> **Scope:** NCI human research biobanks/repositories; not a complete
> diagnostic, high-containment, non-human or accreditation regime.
> **As of:** 2026-01.
> **Review after:** 2027-01-08.

## Actors, stewardship and affected interests

Governance plans precede collection and distinguish sponsors, institutions,
IRBs, investigators, providers, researchers, participants/donors, patient
advocates, communities and the public. Custodian/steward responsibilities,
institutional ownership discussion, conflicts, oversight and access functions
are recommendations—not proof that a resource has assigned or performed them.

> **Claim record — GOV-0012-C04 | source-report**

> **Claim status:** active
> **Scope:** Actor and affected-interest architecture, not universal rights,

## Governance lifecycle and boundary allocation

Governance connects consent/purpose, access, data/material sharing, transfer,
withdrawal limits, sustainability and final transfer/disposition. Informatics
adds identifiers, authorization, access/audit identity and material/data/
location lineage. These controls allocate responsibility at donor→resource,
resource→researcher and sender→receiver boundaries without proving operation.

> **Claim record — GOV-0012-C05 | analytic-judgment**
> **Claim:** The source allocates recommended governance responsibility across
> consent/source→custody, material↔data/identity, permitted-use/access and
> transfer/closeout boundaries through the full human-biobank lifecycle.
> **Claim status:** active
> **Scope:** Governance and boundary design, not legal effectiveness, observed
> compliance or the ability to recall every previously shared data product.
> **As of:** 2026-01.
> **Review after:** 2027-01-08.
> **Evidence status:** inferred
> **Confidence:** high
> **Evidence:** [SRC-0022](../sources/src-0022-nci-best-practices-biospecimen-resources-fourth-edition.md),

> pp. 7–37; §§C.2.8–C.2.10/C.5–C.6, printed pp. 51–70/physical pp. 54–73;

## Quality, audit and accreditation limit

The guide recommends QMS, QA/QC, self-audits, validation, metrics, corrective
action, continuity/recovery testing and review. The separate companion
appendices—including a CAP checklist—are not ingested. References to CAP,
ANAB, ISO or reported adoption of principles are contextual and cannot establish
their current requirements, accreditation status or NCI-guidance effectiveness.

> **Claim record — GOV-0012-C06 | artifact-observation**

> audit, accreditation result, implementation metric or independent
> effectiveness evaluation; the excluded CAP appendix is not current evidence.
