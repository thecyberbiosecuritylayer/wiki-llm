# CAP Biorepository Accreditation Program governance

## Instrument identity and modality

> **Claim record — GOV-0015-C01 | analytic-judgment**
> **Claim:** CAP BAP is a current U.S.-based private voluntary accreditation
> programme for research biorepositories with international participation;
> its scheme requirements apply to enrolled/accredited participants but are
> not U.S. law or a universal biobank mandate.
> **Claim status:** active
> **Scope:** Programme force and geography checked 2026-07-12; not legal advice,
> government deeming authority or a claim about CAP's separate laboratory
> programmes.
> **As of:** 2026-07-12.
> **Review after:** 2026-10-10.
> **Evidence status:** single-source
> **Confidence:** high
> **Evidence:** [SRC-0025](../sources/src-0025-cap-biorepository-accreditation-program.md),
> `SRC-0025-C01/C02/C08`; current programme page opening/`Qualifications`/
> application and worldwide-participation statements; public Standards,
> Preamble/Standard IV, physical pp. 1–2, 5–6.
> **Basis / limits:** Voluntary entry and participant requirements are direct.
> Private accreditation is not converted into statutory force.

## Population, activities and exceptions

> **Claim record — GOV-0015-C02 | source-report**
> **Claim:** BAP applies to research biorepositories and their consent,
> collection, processing, storage, quality, data, release and tracking
> activities, while excluding transplant tissues and retained nonresearch
> clinical samples from BAP scope.
> **Claim status:** active
> **Scope:** CAP programme population/scope; not case-specific eligibility or
> the force of separate transplant/clinical laboratory regimes.
> **As of:** 2026-07-12.
> **Review after:** 2026-10-10.
> **Evidence status:** single-source
> **Confidence:** high
> **Evidence:** [SRC-0025](../sources/src-0025-cap-biorepository-accreditation-program.md),
> `SRC-0025-C03`; current programme page `Qualifications`; public Standards
> Preamble, physical p. 1.
> **Basis / limits:** Included activities and exclusions are direct. An
> exclusion from BAP is not absence of other governance.

> **Claim record — GOV-0015-C03 | source-report**
> **Claim:** CAP's governance model assigns accreditation/inspection authority
> to CAP and compliance responsibility to the governing body, biorepository
> organization, director and qualified personnel across director, physical,
> quality-management and administrative domains.
> **Claim status:** active
> **Scope:** Public scheme actor/control allocation; not observed accountability
> execution or current private checklist completeness.
> **As of:** 2026-07-12.
> **Review after:** 2026-10-10.
> **Evidence status:** single-source
> **Confidence:** high
> **Evidence:** [SRC-0025](../sources/src-0025-cap-biorepository-accreditation-program.md),
> `SRC-0025-C04/C06`; public Standards, Preamble/Standards I–IV and
> Interpretation, physical pp. 1–6.
> **Basis / limits:** Actors and function groups are direct. Private checklist
> clauses are not reconstructed from generic headings.

## Assessment, adoption and exception states

> **Claim record — GOV-0015-C04 | source-report**
> **Claim:** BAP uses a stated two-year on-site peer-inspection cycle with
> off-year self-inspection and corrective-action/review obligations; CAP also
> associates BAP with the dedication of `over 100` biorepositories worldwide
> and separately provides a current searchable accredited-biorepository
> directory.
> **Claim status:** active
> **Scope:** Scheme design and issuer-reported adoption signal; not independent
> prevalence, a complete directory snapshot, positive inspection result or
> effectiveness measure.
> **As of:** 2026-07-12.
> **Review after:** 2026-10-10.
> **Evidence status:** single-source
> **Confidence:** medium
> **Evidence:** [SRC-0025](../sources/src-0025-cap-biorepository-accreditation-program.md),
> `SRC-0025-C05/C08`; current programme page `Peer Inspection Model` and
> closing uptake/directory statements; current directory opening; public
> Standards Standard IV/Interpretation, physical pp. 2, 5–6.
> **Basis / limits:** Programme and uptake signals are direct from CAP but are
> not equated into an exact accreditation count. Wiki records issuer provenance
> and withholds prevalence/effectiveness claims.

> **Claim record — GOV-0015-C05 | analytic-judgment**
> **Claim:** Current CAP presentation makes PT/EQA enrollment optional under
> BAP despite older public-core conditional/participation language; therefore
> PT/EQA is an explicit programme exception, not a universal BAP requirement.
> **Claim status:** active
> **Scope:** Current programme modality versus the November 2016 public core
> document; not private checklist interpretation or an individual certificate
> condition.
> **As of:** 2026-07-12.
> **Review after:** 2026-10-10.
> **Evidence status:** single-source
> **Confidence:** high
> **Evidence:** [SRC-0025](../sources/src-0025-cap-biorepository-accreditation-program.md),
> `SRC-0025-C07`; live `PT/EQA` section; public Standards Standard IV and
> Interpretation I.9/III.D, physical pp. 2–4.
> **Basis / limits:** Current explicit wording controls this representation;
> older tension remains documented rather than silently harmonized.

> completed metrics and independent evaluation.
> **Claim status:** active
> **Scope:** Evidence-state boundary; not a negative finding about CAP or an
> accredited participant.
> **As of:** 2026-07-12.

> **Evidence status:** single-source
