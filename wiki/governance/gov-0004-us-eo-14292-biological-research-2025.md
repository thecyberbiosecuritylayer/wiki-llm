# US Executive Order 14292 nucleic-acid-screening direction 2025

## Status and legal character

Executive Order 14292 is an official presidential directive signed on 5 May
2025 and published on 8 May 2025. Its screening provisions direct executive-
branch officials and agencies, while §9 makes implementation consistent with
applicable law and subject to appropriations. The order is not a statute, a
provider certification, an agency award clause or the updated screening
framework itself.

> **Claim record — GOV-0004-C01 | source-report**
> **Claim:** EO 14292 is a presidential direction to named executive-branch
> officials and relevant agencies, bounded by applicable law, appropriations
> and preserved agency authority rather than a universal private right or
> self-executing provider standard.
> **Claim status:** active
> **Scope:** Governance character of the order's screening-relevant provisions;
> not legal advice or an exhaustive separation-of-powers analysis.
> **As of:** 2026-07-12.
> **Review after:** 2026-08-11.
> **Evidence status:** single-source
> **Confidence:** high
> **Evidence:** [SRC-0013](../sources/src-0013-us-eo-14292-biological-research-2025.md),
> opening authority clause; §§4(b), 5, 7 and 9, 90 FR 19611–19613.
> **Basis / limits:** Directed actors and general boundaries are explicit. The
> exact consequence for a particular award or private entity depends on later
> instruments and applicable law.

## Effect on the 2024 framework record

The order does not silently erase the historical content recorded in
[GOV-0002](gov-0002-us-federally-funded-nucleic-acid-screening-2024.md).
It adds a later governance event: OSTP was directed to revise or replace that
framework within 90 days, connect the updated version to covered federal
procurement and §7 enforcement, and revisit it at least every four years or as
appropriate.

> **As of:** 2025-05-05.
> **Review after:** 2026-08-11.

## Federal and non-federal scope

The order uses two different mechanisms:

| Setting | Directed mechanism | Evidence boundary |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Federally funded life-science procurement | agency procurement through providers/manufacturers adhering to an updated framework | downstream framework and agency terms not in the order |
| Specified non-federally funded research | strategy including action toward comprehensive, scalable and verifiable screening, plus authority-gap proposal | strategy/proposal direction is not a current universal duty |

## Enforcement design

Section 7 directs relevant agencies to use future life-science grant/contract
terms for material compliance, certification, institutional responsibility and
possible funding consequences, consistently with existing laws and
regulations. Section 4(b) tells the updated screening framework to incorporate
those mechanisms.

## Interpretation limits

- The 90- and 180-day periods are source-stated directions; no completion is
  inferred merely because the dates passed.
- `Updated Framework` is a future object in the order, not another name for the
  four-page EO.
- `Revise or replace` does not support choosing `revision`, `replacement` or
  `rescission` without the downstream artifact.
- Federal award/contract enforcement and non-federal strategy/legislation are
  separate implementation paths and must not be collapsed.
- This page does not interpret the legality of a specific grant term or advise
  any organization about compliance.

## Safety boundary

Only actors, modalities, governance mechanisms and evidence-state distinctions
are retained. Detailed biological definitions/outcomes, sequence parameters,
screening-evasion logic, operational contacts and bypass mechanics are omitted.
