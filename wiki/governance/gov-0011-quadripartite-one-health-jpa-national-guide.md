# Quadripartite One Health JPA national implementation governance

## Identity, lineage and current presentation

The 2023 English guide is a joint WHO/FAO/UNEP/WOAH product complementing the
OH JPA 2022–2026. Current custodian pages and March–May 2026 official material
continued to present/use the implementation lineage when checked 2026-07-12.
The renewed Quadripartite MoU runs to 28 November 2030 but is a separate
cooperation-status companion: it neither revises this artifact nor extends the
labelled JPA period by implication.

> **Claim:** The 2023 guide is a jointly issued Quadripartite national-
> implementation guideline still presented/used by official custodians in
> 2026, with renewed cooperation through 2030 kept separate from artifact and
> OH JPA term/version state.
> **Claim status:** active
> **Scope:** Instrument identity/current presentation checked 2026-07-12; not

## Modality and force

The guide repeatedly uses recommendation/adaptation language and is designed
for countries at different stages. It does not itself create a regulated
population, licence, sanction, enforcement authority or national duty. Its
suggestions to adapt law/policy are not enacted legal requirements.

> **Claim record — GOV-0011-C02 | analytic-judgment**
> **Claim:** The guide is nonbinding joint intergovernmental implementation
> guidance; it is not a statute, regulation, licence condition, national
> compliance finding or proof that a country adopted its recommendations.
> **Claim status:** active
> **Scope:** Instrument modality/evidence state, not legal advice or analysis
> of any later country instrument.
> **As of:** 2026-07-12.

## Actor and responsibility architecture

The governance design places primary programme, finance and resource decisions
with national authorities. The Quadripartite may provide support on request
and subject to resources. It recommends national/subnational multisectoral
coordination, a secretariat, technical groups, a budget, stakeholder mapping,
equitable representation and allocated workplan roles. These categories do not
prove a country has institutionalized the mechanism.

## Programme lifecycle and trigger boundary

The five steps define a national programme loop: assess current state, establish
or strengthen coordination, prioritize/cost/plan, implement, then review/share/
learn. The guide allows different entry points and country tailoring. The loop
does not model physical agricultural production, diagnostic custody or trade/
traceability and is not evidence that a state completed any step.

> **Claim record — GOV-0011-C04 | source-report**
> **Claim:** The instrument supplies a flexible five-step governance/programme
> lifecycle with assessment, coordination, planning, implementation and
> review/learning triggers, but no observed national completion or sector-
> product lifecycle.
> **Claim status:** active

## Policy, data and assurance design

The three pathways connect enabling law/policy/financing, institutions and
sector integration, and data/evidence/information systems/knowledge exchange.
Suggested mechanisms include a legal basis for sharing, integrated surveillance
and warning, costed workplans, roles, indicators, baselines, benchmarks,
evaluation, reporting and feedback. These are a useful governance checklist,
not deployed control or effectiveness evidence.

> **Claim record — GOV-0011-C05 | source-report**
> **Claim:** The guide joins law/policy/finance, sector organization and data/
> information-system/MEL governance in one recommended national design, while
> leaving enactment, deployment, completed testing and measured effectiveness
> unestablished.
> **Claim status:** active
> **Scope:** Three pathways and five-step design; not current national law,
> technical architecture, audit result or causal outcome.
> **As of:** 2023-12-06.
> **Review after:** 2027-01-08.

## Reconciliation boundary

This page adds one global guidance lineage. It is materially distinct from the
U.S. NOHF for SF2 scope reconciliation, but the four Quadripartite organizations
do not count as four lineages. Global guidance is not a second national
jurisdiction, and its generic information-system recommendations do not satisfy
the frozen two-jurisdiction animal/plant/environment/cyber-data governance cell.

> adopted, implemented, tested or effective governance.
