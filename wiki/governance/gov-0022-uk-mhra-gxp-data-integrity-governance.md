# UK MHRA GxP data-integrity governance, Revision 1 and current status

## Status and scope

The official page retains MHRA Revision 1, published 2018-03-09 and last
updated 2021-09-27. It is directed to UK industry and public bodies regulated
by MHRA across GLP, GCP, GMP, GDP and GPvP. Section 2 expressly excludes
medical devices. The page/PDF pairing is one MHRA publication, not two
independent governance sources.

> **Claim record — GOV-0022-C01 | source-report**
> **Claim:** MHRA's official record presents Revision 1 as current UK GxP
> data-integrity guidance for MHRA-regulated organizations, with 2018
> publication, 2021 GLP-related wrapper update and an express medical-device
> exclusion.
> **Claim status:** active
> **Scope:** Official status/audience/scope checked 2026-07-12; not every UK
> life-science activity, a substantive post-2018 PDF revision or facility-
> specific applicability.
> **As of:** 2026-07-12.
> **Review after:** 2026-10-10.
> **Evidence status:** single-source
> **Confidence:** high
> **Evidence:** [SRC-0044](../sources/src-0044-uk-mhra-gxp-data-integrity-guidance-2018-current.md),
> `SRC-0044-C01/C02`; GOV.UK header/details/history; PDF p. 3 §§2.1-2.4 and
> p. 21 revision history.
> **Basis / limits:** Dates, regulated audience and exclusion are direct;
> current hosting does not imply a revised PDF.

## Binding character

MHRA calls the document its position and the minimum expectation to achieve
compliance, while directing users to read it with applicable regulations and
GxP-specific guidance. It is interpretive regulator guidance rather than a
self-contained statute or regulation. `Should`, `expected`, `needs` and `must`
language remains tied to that GxP context.

> **Claim record — GOV-0022-C02 | analytic-judgment**
> **Claim:** MHRA Revision 1 is authoritative UK regulator interpretation and
> a stated minimum compliance expectation, but not standalone legislation;
> underlying GxP requirements control binding legal conclusions.
> **Claim status:** active
> **Scope:** Instrument-force distinction; not legal advice or a complete
> mapping of UK/EU pharmaceutical law.
> **As of:** 2026-07-12 review.
> **Review after:** 2026-10-10.
> **Evidence status:** single-source
> **Confidence:** high
> **Evidence:** [SRC-0044](../sources/src-0044-uk-mhra-gxp-data-integrity-guidance-2018-current.md),

## Requirements or recommendations

The instrument expects organizational responsibility, documented data-
integrity controls and rationale, DIRA, risk-proportionate oversight,
periodic audit, CAPA and notification. It covers paper/electronic/hybrid
records, attributed access, administrator separation, retention/recovery,
intended-purpose validation and cloud/service-provider contracts.

> **Claim record — GOV-0022-C03 | source-report**
> **Claim:** MHRA's governance chain links management responsibility and DIRA
> to lifecycle controls, review/audit, attributed access, retention/recovery,
> supplier/cloud agreements, CAPA and significant-incident notification.
> **Claim status:** active
> **Scope:** GxP regulatory expectations; not a complete software architecture,
> inspection finding, compliance result or tested safeguard outcome.
> **As of:** Revision 1 March 2018.
> **Review after:** 2026-10-10.
> **Evidence status:** single-source
> **Confidence:** high
> **Evidence:** [SRC-0044](../sources/src-0044-uk-mhra-gxp-data-integrity-guidance-2018-current.md),

## Exceptions and precedence

The guidance excludes medical devices, does not expect routine forensic data
checking, permits risk-proportionate effort and bounded compensating methods
where systems cannot support individual access. The 2021 official wrapper says
the OECD 2021 data-integrity advisory takes precedence for UK GLP because of
Mutual Acceptance of Data; MHRA guidance remains supplementary for GLP.

> **Claim record — GOV-0022-C04 | source-report**
> **Claim:** MHRA's explicit boundaries include medical-device exclusion,
> no routine-forensic expectation, risk-proportionate/justified compensating
> controls and OECD precedence for the UK GLP branch while MHRA remains
> supplementary.
> **Claim status:** active
> **Scope:** Stated exceptions and GLP precedence; not permission to ignore
> applicable GxP duties or a complete legal hierarchy for every sector.
> **As of:** PDF 2018; official GLP note updated 2021-09-27.
> **Review after:** 2026-10-10.
> **Evidence status:** single-source
> **Confidence:** high
> **Evidence:** [SRC-0044](../sources/src-0044-uk-mhra-gxp-data-integrity-guidance-2018-current.md),
> `SRC-0044-C08`; landing `Good Laboratory Practice` note; PDF p. 3 §2.4,
> p. 4 §3.5, p. 6 §4.4 and pp. 16-17 §6.16.
> **Basis / limits:** Every exception is retained with its branch and
> prerequisite; GLP precedence is not generalized to GMP.

## Adoption, currentness and independence

Current official presentation and the stated regulated audience show
normative availability. The wrapper notes inspection failures and regulatory
actions, but neither artifact measures organization adoption, implementation,
compliance or effectiveness. MHRA is an independent UK inspectorate lineage
from FDA, while its wrapper/PDF and harmonized references remain one lineage.

> **Claim record — GOV-0022-C05 | analytic-judgment**
> **Claim:** The recorded adoption state is current MHRA publication and
> regulator-use context; organization uptake/outcomes remain unknown, and
> MHRA counts as one UK lineage independent from U.S. FDA but not from its own
> wrapper or cited harmonization documents.
> **Claim status:** active

> reconciliation; not a conclusion that organizations have not implemented it.
> **As of:** 2026-07-12.

## Cyberbiosecurity relevance and control mapping

The guidance covers integrity of regulated pharmaceutical data and
computerized workflows, including MRP, industrial automation/PLC, cloud and
provider contexts. It does not identify ERP or QMS-as-software and does not
report deployed control results or independent evaluation.
