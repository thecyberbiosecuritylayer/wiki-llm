# EU European Health Data Space staged governance regime

## Force, current status and temporal boundary

EHDS is an in-force, directly applicable EU regulation. `In force` does not
mean that every duty applies today: Article 105 stages primary-use/EHR and
secondary-use/omics provisions through 2027, 2029, 2031 and 2035. The page
therefore distinguishes legal existence, future applicability, implementing
acts, national preparation, operational deployment and effectiveness.

> **Claim record — GOV-0006-C01 | source-report**
> **Claim:** Regulation (EU) 2025/327 is binding in its entirety and directly
> applicable in all Member States, but its general and subject-specific duties
> follow Article 105's staged dates rather than all applying from entry into
> force on 25 March 2025.
> **Claim status:** active
> **Scope:** EU instrument status checked 2026-07-12; not legal advice or a
> national implementation conclusion.
> **As of:** 2026-07-12.
> **Review after:** 2026-10-10.
> **Evidence status:** single-source
> **Confidence:** high
> **Evidence:** [SRC-0016](../sources/src-0016-eu-ehds-regulation-2025.md),
> Article 105, PDF pp. 90–91; EUR-Lex dates/status checked 2026-07-12.
> **Basis / limits:** Binding character and staged dates are direct. Readiness,
> national safeguards and implementing-act completeness require later records.

## Governance modalities

EHDS uses distinct modalities:

- natural-person rights and healthcare-provider/professional duties for
  primary use;
- EHR manufacturer/importer/distributor conformity and market-surveillance
  duties;
- health data holder, access body and user duties for secondary use;
- permit, health-data-request, prohibited-use and opt-out/exception mechanisms;
- `MyHealth@EU` and `HealthData@EU` cross-border infrastructures;
- public catalogues, activity reports, complaints, enforcement and future
  evaluation.

> **Claim record — GOV-0006-C02 | analytic-judgment**
> **Claim:** EHDS governance separates primary-use rights, EHR-product
> conformity, secondary-use permit/prohibition and cross-border infrastructure
> modalities, so an interoperability label, permit purpose, audit requirement
> or future implementing act cannot be treated as complete compliance.
> **Claim status:** active
> **Scope:** Structural governance interpretation of the Regulation.
> **As of:** 2026-07-12.
> **Review after:** 2026-10-10.
> **Evidence status:** inferred
> **Confidence:** high
> **Evidence:** [SRC-0016](../sources/src-0016-eu-ehds-regulation-2025.md),
> Articles 1–24, 25–49, 50–81 and 92–105, PDF pp. 29–91.
> **Basis / limits:** Each modality and its actor set is direct; the compact
> lifecycle is editorial. Applicable implementing/national rules can add detail.

## Actors and accountability dependencies

Natural persons, health professionals/providers, digital health authorities,
health data access bodies, contact points, holders, users, trusted holders,
EHR manufacturers/importers/distributors, market-surveillance authorities,
Member States, the Commission and EHDS Board have differentiated roles.
Controller/processor allocation changes by processing stage rather than being
assigned once to an entire infrastructure.

> **Claim record — GOV-0006-C03 | source-report**
> **Claim:** EHDS assigns differentiated institutional, national, supplier/
> service and cross-border responsibilities across primary use, EHR products
> and secondary use, including stage-specific controllership and compliance-
> check responsibilities.
> **Claim status:** active

## Rights, purposes, prohibitions and exceptions

Primary-use rights and optional restrictions/opt-outs differ from secondary-
use permits and opt-out. Secondary-use purposes, prohibited uses, public-
interest override conditions, Member State additional safeguards, IP/trade-
secret protection and third-country participation each have distinct tests.

> **Claim record — GOV-0006-C04 | source-report**
> **Claim:** EHDS preserves purpose-, role- and stage-specific rights,
> prohibitions, opt-outs, exceptions and national safeguard options rather than
> creating one unconditional right to access all electronic health data.
> **Claim status:** active
> **Scope:** Regulation-level boundary; not adjudication of a request, permit or
> Member State law.
> **As of:** 2026-07-12.
> **Review after:** 2026-10-10.
> **Evidence status:** single-source

## Assurance and implementation boundary

EHDS mandates conformity documentation, testing-environment assessment,
market surveillance, secure-environment audit, public reporting, penalties and
future evaluation. These are assurance designs. The source does not contain a
Member State readiness census, product test result, third-party audit outcome,
serious-incident case or causal-effectiveness evaluation.

> **Claim record — GOV-0006-C05 | analytic-judgment**
> **Claim:** EHDS supplies binding assurance, enforcement and evaluation design
> but no completed implementation/effectiveness record for the represented
> controls; one EHDS Board operating act does not close that evidence gap.
> **Claim status:** active
> **Scope:** Evidence maturity through the bounded official check on
> 2026-07-12.
> **As of:** 2026-07-12.
> **Review after:** 2026-10-10.
> **Evidence status:** inferred

> 75–76; Articles 99–102, PDF pp. 88–89; `SRC-0016-C10`.
> **Basis / limits:** Duties and future metrics are direct. Results require
> separate primary national, product, audit, regulator or evaluation records.
