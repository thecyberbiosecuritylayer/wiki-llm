# U.S. FDA drug-CGMP data-integrity governance, final 2018 guidance

## Status and scope

FDA presents *Data Integrity and Compliance With Drug CGMP: Questions and
Answers* as final Level 1 guidance, issued in December 2018 under docket
`FDA-2018-D-3984`. The retained live page labels its content current as of
2018-12-13 and names CVM, CBER and CDER. The drug-CGMP scope expressly includes
biologics; it is not a general medical-device or all-manufacturing instrument.

## Binding character

The guidance states FDA's current thinking, creates no rights and is not
binding on FDA or the public. An alternative approach may be used if it
satisfies applicable statutes and regulations. Separately, FDA cites the
FD&C Act and 21 CFR Parts 210, 211 and 212 as binding bases for drug-CGMP
record, control, inspection and data-integrity duties. Recommendation language
and cited legal requirements must not be collapsed into one force class.

## Requirements and recommendations

The cited CGMP basis covers complete/contemporaneous records, exact and
complete secure backups, original or true-copy retention, authorized changes
and review. FDA recommendations add risk-based system/control design,
intended-workflow validation, administrator independence, audit-trail review,
investigation and CAPA. MES, LIMS, electronic batch records and cloud
infrastructure are explicit examples, not proof of a complete enterprise
architecture or deployment.

> **Claim record — GOV-0021-C03 | source-report**
> **Claim:** FDA maps cited CGMP record duties and risk-based recommendations
> across data lifecycle, backup, intended-workflow validation, access,
> audit-trail review, investigation and remediation.
> **Claim status:** active
> **Scope:** Drug-CGMP governance expectations; not compliance, validation or

## Exceptions and interpretation limits

FDA permits an alternative approach that satisfies applicable law. Additional
bounded cases include shared read-only accounts only for viewing, controlled
electronic signatures, risk-derived audit frequency where regulation gives no
frequency, and static records only when original/true-copy content and meaning
are preserved. Invalid results require scientifically sound justification and
retention of original data.

> **Claim record — GOV-0021-C04 | source-report**
> **Claim:** FDA's exceptions are conditional rather than blanket: alternative
> approaches must satisfy law, viewing-only access cannot perform attributable

> record-control requirements.
> **Claim status:** active

## Adoption, currentness and enforcement boundary

Final issuance, current official hosting and FDA's report of inspection
findings/regulatory actions establish normative availability and enforcement
context. They do not establish organization-level adoption, implementation,
compliance, remediation success or safeguard effectiveness. The FDA guidance
is a U.S. jurisdictional lineage independent from UK MHRA authorship, although
both draw on international GxP concepts.

## Cyberbiosecurity relevance and control mapping

The instrument governs integrity of biologics/drug manufacturing data and
computerized workflows, including records that influence batch conformance
and release. It does not prescribe a complete OT cyber architecture, name QMS
software/ERP, report a cyber incident or evaluate a deployed safeguard.
