# UK synthetic nucleic-acid screening guidance 2024

## Status and scope

DSIT published this instrument as `Guidance` on 8 October 2024. It sets
baseline expectations and expected standards of responsible practice for
individuals and organizations involved in provision, use and transfer of
synthetic nucleic acids and relevant benchtop equipment while operating in the
UK. It is voluntary normative guidance, not a statute or a universal assertion
that every referenced control has been adopted.

The canonical page remained live and substantively unchanged on 2026-07-12.
The page's public date fields and visible history show no substantive update
after publication; a later internal publishing-system timestamp is not treated
as a policy revision.

## Defined terms and actors

The instrument distinguishes:

- synthetic nucleic acid as the relevant material class;
- customer user, principal user and end user as different roles;
- providers that synthesize/distribute material;
- benchtop manufacturers and relevant equipment;
- third-party vendors involved in resale or equipment distribution; and
- a policy concern category used to trigger screening/review.

## Binding character

The guidance uses recommendation language such as `should`, `encouraged` and
`intended to assist`. Its recommended screening lifecycle is therefore recorded
as voluntary expected responsible practice.

The page also summarizes existing statutes, regulations, licensing,
notification, occupational-risk, data-protection and export-control duties.
Those instruments may bind independently within their own scopes. Their
primary texts, amendments, exceptions and authoritative interpretations were
not ingested here, so a guidance sentence using `must` is not converted into an
independently verified legal obligation for every provider or user.

## Requirements or recommendations

The safe policy model is:

1. identify accountable provider/vendor/manufacturer/user roles;
2. receive an order or equipment request with sufficient identity and purpose
   context;
3. apply customer and order screening;
4. conduct accountable follow-up review when concerns arise;
5. fulfill, or refuse and report through the appropriate policy channel;
6. retain protected decision and interaction records;
7. preserve legitimate-user and transfer accountability through equipment and
   material lifecycle changes; and
8. protect customer, intellectual-property, order, screening and decision data.

## Cyberbiosecurity relevance

The policy operates at a digital-to-physical boundary: digital order, identity,
screening and accountable-review states condition whether a physical product
is fulfilled or synthesis equipment becomes available. The same control
environment holds personal/organizational identity, intellectual property,
screening records and protected screening capability, creating simultaneous
privacy, information-security and biosecurity requirements.

## Exceptions and interpretation limits

- Voluntary guidance does not require a framework-wide statutory exception
  process analogous to a binding licensing rule.
- The document's definitions and referenced legal regimes contain material-
  and context-specific boundaries; their operational detail is not reproduced.
- Legal notification, licensing, data-protection and export requirements must
  be checked against current primary instruments before application.
- Recommendations to users with in-house synthesis capability do not establish
  that every internal research collaboration is a provider transaction.
- A UK recommendation cannot be generalized to US or global procurement, and
  similarity with `GOV-0002` is not proof of legal equivalence.
- This page is defensive governance analysis, not legal advice.

## Safety and privacy boundary

This page preserves accountability, lifecycle and security objectives without
consolidating screening-evasion knowledge. Exact sequence windows or matching
logic, named examples, transaction-indicator lists, detailed identity
checklists, reporting contacts and device/reagent bypass or tamper mechanics
are withheld. This does not reduce the evidence needed to map preventive,
detective, response and record controls.
