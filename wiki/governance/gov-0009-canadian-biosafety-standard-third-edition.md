# Canadian Biosafety Standard Third Edition governance and licence-condition model

## Current instrument and lineage

CBS remains titled Third Edition 2022, published November 2022, effective
1 April 2023 and carrying a December 2024 minor update. It superseded the 2015
Second Edition. PHAC says the current Third Edition continues to apply pending
alignment to the HPTA amendments of 26 March 2026. The separate Biosecurity
Addendum has applied to the specified RG4/CL4 population since 5 January 2026;
it is not silently folded into this page's base-instrument claims.

## Modality and binding character

PHAC/CFIA distinguish five levels: enabling Acts/regulations; instruments in
support of legislation; biosafety requirements; policy documents; and
recommendation/science-oriented guidance/technical documents. CBS is a
national standard in the `Biosafety Requirements` tier, not itself an Act or
regulation. Its applicable requirements become mandatory conditions of HPTA
licences and relevant HAR permits.

> **Claim record — GOV-0009-C02 | source-report**
> **Claim:** CBS is a national standard/biosafety-requirement instrument below
> enabling legislation, and its mandatory force for regulated parties is
> mediated through applicable licence/permit conditions rather than a claim
> that the standard itself is a statute.
> **Claim status:** active

## Regulated scope and exceptions

The base standard covers facilities handling or storing specified regulated
human and terrestrial-animal pathogens/toxins. It differentiates other CFIA
aquatic-animal and plant-pest standards, excludes specified natural-environment
primary specimens/detection-only activity from HPTA scope, and recognizes
bounded diagnostic/veterinary licence exemptions. Excluded, exempt and
reference-only uses are not equivalent categories.

> **Claim record — GOV-0009-C03 | source-report**
> **Claim:** CBS governance differentiates regulated, excluded, licence-exempt
> and reference-only populations and preserves separate aquatic-animal/
> plant-pest instruments instead of generalizing one laboratory rule.
> **Claim status:** active
> **Scope:** Source categories in §§1.1–1.2; no specimen/facility classification
> or conclusion about other legal duties.
> **As of:** 2026-07-12.
> **Review after:** 2026-08-11.

## Programme, control and assurance governance

The standard requires risk assessments, a biosafety programme/manual,
biosecurity plan, training, incident response, records and performance/
verification testing. Requirement 4.1.8 joins biological-material
accountability/inventory to physical/personnel security, information management,
incident response and training/evaluation under senior approval. These are
normative design and assurance requirements.

> **Claim record — GOV-0009-C05 | source-report**
> **Claim:** CBS maps institutional governance to inventory/accountability,
> access/security, information management, incident response, evaluation and
> performance/verification testing, but the instrument itself supplies no
> facility implementation, completed test, metric or effectiveness result.
> **Claim status:** active
> **Scope:** Governance/control design and evidence-state boundary; not a

## International alignment without equivalence

CBS directly cites WHO Laboratory Biosafety Manual 4 and WOAH risk-based
approaches, then maps WHO's core/heightened/maximum concept to Canada's
graduated containment levels. This is alignment, not identical terminology or
automatic cross-compliance. The wiki's WHO Laboratory Biosecurity Guidance
2024 is a separate WHO instrument; comparison to it is editorial, not the
direct cross-reference stated by CBS.

> **Claim record — GOV-0009-C06 | analytic-judgment**
> **Claim:** Canada supplies an additional national adoption context aligned
> with global risk-based principles while retaining distinct scope, force,
> exceptions and containment/licence mechanisms.
> **Claim status:** active
> **Scope:** Canada–global governance reconciliation; not equivalence with WHO
> guidance, WOAH adoption or a complete international crosswalk.
> **As of:** 2026-07-12.
> **Review after:** 2026-08-11.
> **Evidence status:** inferred
> **Confidence:** high

> full CBS reference/title check distinguishing LBM4 (2020) from the differently

> **Basis / limits:** CBS's LBM4/WOAH alignment is direct; reconciliation with
