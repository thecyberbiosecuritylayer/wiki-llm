# US National One Health Framework 2025–2029 governance

## Current instrument and status

CDC, USDA and DOI jointly released NOHF-Zoonoses on 10 January 2025. CDC's
current coordination page, dated 15 April 2026, still presented the framework
as guiding collaborative federal efforts over five years when checked
2026-07-12. The current PDF has July 2025 file metadata but states no formal
revision, so this page does not manufacture one.

## Modality and force

The framework responds to congressional direction and sets a federal policy/
coordination structure, mission, vision, principles, goals and objectives. It
does not prescribe its own sanctions, licences, regulated population or legal-
compliance mechanism. Words such as `support`, `develop`, `strengthen` and
`encourage` remain programme objectives, not completed duties/results.

> **Claim record — GOV-0010-C02 | analytic-judgment**

> **Claim status:** active

## Coordination and actor architecture

The OHCU supplies a standing federal platform with shared CDC/DOI/USDA
leadership and a rotating chair. It is intended to coordinate with other
federal agencies and state, Tribal, local, territorial, academic,
nongovernmental, private, affected-community, public and international
partners. It does not create a formal rights code or complete operational RACI.

> **Claim record — GOV-0010-C03 | source-report**
> **Claim:** The governance design assigns federal coordination/shared
> leadership to OHCU/CDC/DOI/USDA and maps broad governmental and non-
> governmental partner classes, while leaving entity-level duties, rights and
> performance unestablished.
> **Claim status:** active
> **Scope:** Framework actor/governance design, not observed participation,

## Scope and programme lifecycle

The framework covers people, domestic/wild animals, plants and the environment
but focuses primarily on zoonoses/public-health preparedness. Its seven goals
trace coordination, prevention, preparedness, investigation/response/recovery,
surveillance, laboratory and workforce functions. The lifecycle does not
replace farm production, animal/plant movement, trade/traceability or other
sector-specific governance.

> **Claim record — GOV-0010-C04 | analytic-judgment**
> **Claim:** NOHF-Zoonoses supplies a bounded U.S. One Health governance/
> emergency-programme lifecycle but not a complete agriculture production,
> traceability, environmental or cross-border governance regime.
> **Claim status:** active

> **As of:** 2026-07-12.

## Data, laboratory and assurance governance

Goals 3–6 call for exercises/AARs, models, coordinated surveillance, secure
sharing/interoperability, harmonized sample protocols, validated assay design,
reporting and laboratory biosafety/biosecurity. They do not provide a deployed
system map, completed exercise/validation, metric or independent evaluation.

> **Claim record — GOV-0010-C05 | source-report**
> **Claim:** The framework joins surveillance/data/model, laboratory/sample and
> exercise/AAR governance objectives but contains only design/future-work
> evidence, not implementation, testing results or measured effectiveness.
> **Claim status:** active
> **Scope:** Goals 3–6; not a current technical architecture or finding that no
> partner has implemented these functions elsewhere.
> **As of:** 2026-07-12.
> **Review after:** 2026-10-10.
> **Evidence status:** single-source
> **Confidence:** high

## Coverage contribution and unresolved reconciliation

One coordinated U.S. government lineage strengthens One Health scope, actor,
lifecycle, surveillance/laboratory and governance context. It does not meet
SF2/SF3 independence or supply farm/plant/veterinary-lab architecture,
cyber-biological paths, incidents, tested controls or a second jurisdiction.
