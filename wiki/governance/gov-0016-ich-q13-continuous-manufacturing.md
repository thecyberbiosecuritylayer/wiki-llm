# ICH Q13 continuous-manufacturing governance and regional adoption

## Status and scope

ICH Q13 is a harmonized scientific/regulatory guideline for development,
implementation, operation and lifecycle management of continuous manufacture
of drug substances and drug products. It reached ICH Step 4 on 2022-11-16.
The represented regional records are:

| Context | Recorded state | Date and force boundary |
| --- | --- | --- |
| ICH | Final Step 4 guideline recommended for adoption by regulatory bodies of ICH regions | adopted 2022-11-16; not a self-executing global law |
| United States FDA | `Final`, `Level 1 Guidance` | issued March 2023/content current 2023-03-01; PDF expressly says nonbinding recommendations and permits a compliant alternative approach |
| European Union EMA | `Adopted` Step 5 scientific guideline, reference `EMA/CHMP/ICH/427817/2021` | final CHMP adoption 2022-12-15; recorded coming-into-effect/legal-effective date 2023-07-10; exact legal applicability still depends on the EU/regional framework |

## Defined terms and applicability

Q13 defines a continuous-manufacturing system through continuously fed inputs,
in-process transformation and concomitant output across at least two directly
connected unit operations. It covers chemical entities and therapeutic
proteins, new products and conversion from approved batch processes. It says
its principles may apply to other biological/biotechnological entities; this
is not automatic inclusion of ATMP, industrial fermentation, food or other
biomanufacturing variants.

## Binding character

Q13 uses normative `should` language and links its recommendations to regional
requirements. FDA expressly states its guidance is not binding on FDA or the
public and that an alternative approach may be used if it satisfies applicable
law. The EMA record's `legal effective date` is preserved exactly, but this
page does not transform a scientific guideline into a directly applicable EU
regulation or claim identical enforcement across Member States.

## Requirements or recommendations

Q13's governance chain asks a manufacturer/applicant to justify and maintain:

- development and control strategy for connected continuous operations;
- input/material, equipment, digital-interface and process-dynamics controls;
- monitoring, model, sampling, traceability, diversion and disposition logic;
- reference/offline testing where real-time release testing is insufficient;
- process/model validation or justified continuous process verification;
- CTD evidence, site-accessible model information and PQS-managed procedures;
- investigation, root-cause analysis, CAPA and lifecycle/change management.

Q13 says manufacturers should seek regulatory approval before implementing a
conversion of an approved batch process to continuous manufacture. It also
says process-validation requirements are established by region. Q13 points to
M4Q, Q1A/Q5C/Q5E/Q6A and Q7–Q12, but those cited instruments are not
independently ingested through this page.

## Cyberbiosecurity relevance and control mappings

Q13 addresses the quality side of a coupled digital/material process and calls
for documentation of relevant digital control interfaces, monitoring/model
data, control decisions and material-quality gates. It does not prescribe
cybersecurity identity, access, network segmentation, incident response,
backup or trusted digital recovery. Those functions need separate OT-cyber
evidence and reconciliation with Q13 quality/disposition recommendations and
regional requirements.

The exact quality-control family is
[CTL-0015](../controls/ctl-0015-continuous-manufacturing-quality-control.md). Generic
OT controls remain in
[CTL-0011](../controls/ctl-0011-ot-defense-in-depth-resilience-assurance.md). A
crosswalk between them is not evidence of adoption, compliance or
effectiveness.

## Exceptions and interpretation limits

- Annex examples are illustrative and alternative approaches are allowed.
- Q13 says real-time release testing is not a regulatory requirement for
  continuous manufacture; when RTRT is proposed, it calls for a reference
  method, data-lapse risk assessment and alternative/additional controls as
  appropriate.
- Technical FDA/EMA copies are one ICH lineage. They do not meet a two-
  jurisdiction independent-evidence floor for `BMO-GR` by themselves.
- An apparent FDA-only final cross-reference defect in Annex V §3.3 is recorded
  in `SRC-0031`; the matching ICH/EMA wording and local section context identify
  Annex V §3.2 as the intended target, and the error is not silently normalized.
- No implementation, inspection, approval, enforcement, test metric, incident
  or outcome is supplied.
