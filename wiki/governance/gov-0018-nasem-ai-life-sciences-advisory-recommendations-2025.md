# NASEM AI in life sciences advisory recommendations 2025

## Status and scope

This page records the formal conclusions/recommendations of the 2025 NASEM
consensus study *The Age of AI in the Life Sciences*. The official project is
marked completed and the publication record remained available when checked on
2026-07-12. The report was requested/sponsored by DoD, but its committee advice
is not a DoD rule or adopted executive policy.

The scope is U.S. research, surveillance, data/compute stewardship,
countermeasure preparedness and capability evaluation, with international
cooperation mentioned for biosurveillance. It is not a statute, regulation,
procurement clause, appropriation, interagency implementation plan or two-
jurisdiction governance reconciliation.

## Recommended governance portfolio

| Portfolio | Named actors | Advisory action | Evidence boundary |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Foundational biological research and biosurveillance | DoD, HHS/CDC, USDA, DOE, DHS and other research agencies | continue infectious-agent research and invest in surveillance networks with global public-health cooperation | programme recommendation, not a surveillance deployment or result |
| AI-enabled countermeasure development | HHS/NIH/BARDA/ARPA-H and DoD/DARPA/Chemical and Biological Defense | fund biology-specific AI design approaches for emergency countermeasure work | no award, approval, product or outcome is demonstrated |
| Emergency compute/tool partnership | BARDA, DOE and DoD with private partners | establish continuously accessible AI/compute capacity that can activate during an emergency | no established partnership, capacity or exercise result in this package |
| Capability-triggered risk governance | DoD and the U.S. AI Safety Institute named by the report | continually connect data availability/quality to emerging capability, benchmarks, thresholds and sandbox assessment | report-era actor/policy context is not automatically current in 2026 |
| AI-compatible biological data | federal research funders and public/private partners | create/steward accessible training sets and biology-specific tools | accessible does not mean unrestricted, implemented or representative |
| Provenance and repository stewardship | agencies housing/funding biological databases; NAIRR pilot | treat databases as strategic assets, improve provenance/curation/maintenance and explore central repositories/dedicated stewardship | target design, not a completed repository/governance outcome |

## Capability and risk governance logic

The committee's `if–then` approach treats high-quality biological-data
availability and emerging model capability as signals for continual
reassessment. It calls for benchmarks, metrics, thresholds and sandboxed
evaluation anchored in broader capability, actor and mitigation context. It
does not publish binding thresholds, a current assessment protocol or a result.

> **Claim record — GOV-0018-C03 | source-report**
> **Claim:** NASEM recommends continual, condition-triggered assessment of data
> availability/quality and emerging biological-AI capability, using benchmarks,
> metrics, thresholds and possible sandbox evaluation rather than a one-time
> static forecast.
> **Claim status:** active
> **Scope:** Governance design principle; no sensitive threshold value,
> implementation, assessment result or legal trigger is represented.
> **As of:** 2025-04-23.

orders, national-security memoranda, NIH/USDA/HHS programmes or screening
frameworks describe the report's evidence/policy context. They are not
incorporated current-law texts and do not establish their July 2026 status.
