# ISO 20387 biobanking standard governance

## Current instrument and successor boundary

> **Claim record — GOV-0014-C01 | source-report**
> **Claim:** As of 2026-07-12, ISO 20387:2018 Edition 1 remains the published
> International Standard at stage 90.92, while Edition 2 is only an
> under-development ISO/FDIS at stage 50.00 that is expected to replace it.
> **Claim status:** active
> **Scope:** Official ISO lifecycle status; not a forecast of publication date,
> final Edition 2 wording or a claim that the 2018 edition is withdrawn.
> **As of:** 2026-07-12.
> **Review after:** 2026-07-26.
> **Evidence status:** single-source
> **Confidence:** high
> **Evidence:** [SRC-0024](../sources/src-0024-iso-20387-biobanking-standard-current-status.md),
> `SRC-0024-C01/C02/C04`; ISO pages 67888 and 88703, title/header, General
> information and Life cycle.
> **Basis / limits:** ISO's own stage labels are direct. Future replacement is
> not treated as completed publication at this cutoff.

## Scope, users and exclusions

> **Claim record — GOV-0014-C02 | source-report**
> **Claim:** The current public abstract scopes the standard to competence,
> impartiality and consistent operation of research/development biobanks for
> human, animal, fungal, plant and microorganism material/data; it excludes
> food/feed production and therapeutic use and sends diagnostic/treatment human
> material first to ISO 15189 and other clinical standards.
> **Claim status:** active
> **Scope:** Abstract-level international scope/exceptions; not organization-
> specific applicability, legal advice or clause-level normative analysis.
> **As of:** 2026-07-12.
> **Review after:** 2026-07-26.
> **Evidence status:** single-source
> **Confidence:** high
> **Evidence:** [SRC-0024](../sources/src-0024-iso-20387-biobanking-standard-current-status.md),
> `SRC-0024-C02/C03/C06`; ISO 20387:2018 Abstract/Notes and ISO 15189:2022
> public metadata/Abstract.
> **Basis / limits:** Scope/exclusions are direct public metadata. Full clauses
> and jurisdiction-specific priority remain outside the artifact.

> **Claim record — GOV-0014-C03 | analytic-judgment**
> **Claim:** ISO 20387 supplies an international competence standard usable by
> biobanks, users, regulators, peer-assessment schemes and accreditation bodies,
> but does not itself prove legal incorporation, a jurisdictional accreditation
> programme, adoption, conformity or implementation.
> **Claim status:** active
> **Scope:** Instrument modality and actor/use classes; not a universal
> voluntary-status claim where another authority has incorporated the standard.
> **As of:** 2026-07-12.
> **Review after:** 2026-07-26.
> **Evidence status:** single-source
> **Confidence:** high
> **Evidence:** [SRC-0024](../sources/src-0024-iso-20387-biobanking-standard-current-status.md),
> `SRC-0024-C02/C03/C07`; ISO 20387:2018 Abstract/Note 1.
> **Basis / limits:** Intended users and standard status are direct; force and
> adoption require a separate jurisdiction/scheme predicate.

## Revision and diagnostic reconciliation

> **Claim record — GOV-0014-C04 | artifact-observation**
> **Claim:** The public Edition 2 FDIS abstract contains visible scope wording
> changes, but draft text and omitted abstract wording are not current final
> requirements and cannot silently alter the 2018 edition at the cutoff.
> **Claim status:** active
> **Scope:** Temporal/version control; not full redline analysis or a claim
> about requirements hidden in the paid texts.
> **As of:** 2026-07-12.
> **Review after:** 2026-07-26.
> **Evidence status:** single-source
> **Confidence:** high
> **Evidence:** [SRC-0024](../sources/src-0024-iso-20387-biobanking-standard-current-status.md),
> `SRC-0024-C04/C05`; comparison of the two public abstracts and lifecycle
> states.
> **Basis / limits:** Visible metadata differences and draft status are direct;
> legal/operational consequences are not inferred.

> **Claim record — GOV-0014-C05 | analytic-judgment**
> **Claim:** Current ISO metadata preserves a functional governance boundary:
> ISO 20387 addresses research/development biobanking, whereas ISO 15189:2022
> is the published medical-laboratory competence standard and is identified as
> primary for diagnostic/treatment human-material entities.
> **Claim status:** active
> **Scope:** International standard-family scope reconciliation; not full
> compliance mapping or proof of accreditation under either standard.
> **As of:** 2026-07-12.
> **Review after:** 2026-07-26.
> **Evidence status:** single-source
> **Confidence:** high
> **Evidence:** [SRC-0024](../sources/src-0024-iso-20387-biobanking-standard-current-status.md),
> `SRC-0024-C03/C06`; ISO 20387:2018 Abstract Note 2 and ISO 15189:2022
> title/Abstract/status.
> **Basis / limits:** Source-specific scope branches are explicit. Both pages
> remain one ISO lineage and cannot manufacture SF2 independence.

> **Claim record — GOV-0014-C06 | analytic-judgment**
> **Claim:** `GOV-0014` fills the current international biobank-standard and
> exception gap but leaves `LAB-GR` Partial until independent national/
> accreditation adoption evidence is ingested and reconciled; no
> implementation/effectiveness or global gate changes.
> **Claim status:** active
> **Scope:** Frozen-rubric sufficiency immediately after SRC-0024; not a
> prediction about any pending accreditation transaction.
> **As of:** 2026-07-12.
> **Review after:** 2026-07-26.
> **Evidence status:** single-source
> **Confidence:** high
> **Evidence:** `GOV-0014-C01`–`C05`; frozen `LAB-GR` criterion in
> [SYN-0001](../syntheses/syn-0001-coverage-rubric.md); current
> [SYN-0010](../syntheses/syn-0010-laboratory-biobank-scope-asset-lifecycle-reconciliation.md).
> **Basis / limits:** One international-standard lineage cannot satisfy the
> still-missing adoption/accreditation reconciliation predicate.
