# HHS synthetic-nucleic-acid procurement award condition

> HHS GPS Version 2.0 supplies a current, bounded department-level award-policy
> implementation of a synthetic-nucleic-acid procurement condition. It is not
> a universal U.S. law, does not cover NIH awards, and does not demonstrate
> recipient/provider compliance or screening effectiveness.

## Status and scope

The HHS Grants Policy Statement (`GPS`) is an agency grants-policy instrument.
Version 2.0 is effective 1 October 2025 and applies to covered awards and
funding-adding modifications from that date. A technical edit published
18 December 2025 retained the same effective date. Appendix D section
`D.5.2.3` states a synthetic-nucleic-acid/equipment procurement condition from
26 April 2025. The retained text does not state a separate original publication
date for Version 2.0, so the page records only the known technical-edit and
effective dates.

The different dates describe different predicates: 26 April 2025 is the date
stated for the subject-matter condition; 1 October 2025 is the applicability
date of GPS Version 2.0. This page does not silently resolve how predecessor
GPS versions or individual award amendments operated between those dates.

## Binding character

HHS says the GPS is incorporated by reference in the official Notice of Award
(`NoA`) as a standard term and condition, that the NoA contains the terms of
the particular award, and that accepted NoA contents are binding. The GPS also
places noncompliance within its monitoring and remedy framework. This is a
binding-award-relationship mechanism within the stated scope, not a statute or
general rule for every U.S. purchaser.

## Applicability and duty holders

The policy distinguishes:

- HHS agencies issuing covered awards;
- recipients responsible for the award and its work;
- subrecipients receiving applicable flow-down terms;
- providers or manufacturers from which covered procurement is made; and
- NIH awards, which are expressly outside this HHS GPS and use the NIH GPS.

Version 2.0 applies to discretionary recipients except NIH for awards or
funding-adding modifications on or after 1 October 2025. It generally excludes
awards to individuals and, other than Appendix D, non-discretionary awards,
subject to the policy's stated agency discretion. Applicable terms flow to
subawards unless a policy or award term says otherwise.

## Procurement requirement

At safe abstraction, `D.5.2.3` requires covered scientific-research awards to
include a condition that recipients adhere to the OSTP screening framework and
use HHS funds to procure covered synthetic-nucleic-acid materials/equipment
from adhering sources. HHS says the framework requirement applies to all work
performed under the award.

The referenced 2024 OSTP framework separately describes provider/manufacturer
adherence through high-level screening, legitimacy review, reporting, record
and information-security functions. The HHS GPS adopts the procurement
condition; it does not reproduce an independent conformity method or verify
provider performance.

## Currency and successor-framework reconciliation

The current GPS still cites EO 14110 and the 2024 OSTP framework. EO 14292,
signed later, directs OSTP to revise or replace that framework and directs
funding agencies toward adherence to the updated framework. The retained EO
does not itself contain the successor, and the current GPS does not identify a
completed successor.

Accordingly, this page records the current HHS award condition and its literal
2024-framework reference while leaving the successor's text, date and
relationship to this condition unresolved. It does not label the HHS GPS void,
the 2024 framework fully current, or the ordered successor completed.

> **Claim record — GOV-0028-C05 | analytic-judgment**

## Control and workflow mappings

| Policy edge | Defensive control/workflow function | Evidence boundary |
| --- | --- | --- |
| award/recipient → procurement condition | make funding context and accountable recipient explicit before covered purchase | policy term, not an actual NoA |
| recipient → provider/manufacturer selection | use HHS funds with a source adhering to the referenced framework | adherence assertion/conformity method not shown |
| order → screen/review/decision | referenced framework supplies the high-level screening and accountable-review functions | HHS GPS does not measure their operation |
| decision → record/report | referenced framework preserves decision/report/record functions | no HHS audit or enforcement instance |
| recipient → subrecipient | applicable terms flow through written subaward agreements | clause population and compliance unknown |

These mappings connect to

effective control evidence.

## Exceptions and interpretation limits

- NIH awards are excluded from this HHS GPS and must be assessed against the
  separate NIH GPS.
- Version 2.0's date boundary applies to awards/modifications adding funds from
  1 October 2025; the page does not reconstruct every predecessor award state.
- Non-discretionary and individual-award treatment is limited as stated in
  §1.2, with Appendix D and agency-discretion qualifications preserved.
- The general flow-down rule does not prove that a particular subaward
  agreement carried a correct `D.5.2.3` clause.
- “Adhering source” is a policy predicate here, not independent conformity,
  technical performance or effectiveness evidence.
- General noncompliance remedies are not evidence that a nucleic-acid case was
  investigated or sanctioned.

## Evidence and uncertainty

The HHS source was retained as complete, deterministic normalized text because
direct official endpoints returned Akamai `403` to the shell. This supports
reproducible content review but not official upstream byte identity. The
instrument is one HHS lineage; the PDF and live-page representations do not
create source independence. EO 14292 is independent for the later executive
direction, not for implementation or effectiveness of this HHS condition.

## Safety boundary

Only force, scope, actor, award, procurement and high-level control predicates
are represented. Exact screening parameters, biological examples, concern
criteria, reporting contacts and any evasion or bypass detail are omitted.
