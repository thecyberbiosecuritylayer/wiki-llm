# US federally funded nucleic-acid synthesis screening framework 2024

## Status and legal character

This page records the **as-issued September 2024 design** of the NSTC/OSTP
framework. It told federal life-sciences funding agencies to establish covered
procurement requirements under applicable authority, including through award
terms, and stated that relevant purchases on or after 26 April 2025 would be
covered. It encouraged broader use but did not present itself as a statute or a
universal rule for all private procurement.

The later status is now **partially reconciled**. EO 14292 directed OSTP to
revise or replace this 2024 framework and connect the updated framework to
covered procurement and future enforcement. The order did not itself supply
the successor. This page therefore does not say that the original terms remain
operative, were wholly rescinded, or were implemented uniformly. GOV-0028 now
documents one bounded current HHS award-policy implementation excluding NIH;
the completed successor and wider agency/award states remain separate gaps.

## Covered roles and procurement boundary

The framework distinguishes:

- federal agencies that fund life-sciences research;
- federally funded customers/recipients making covered purchases;
- providers that synthesize and distribute nucleic acids as a service;
- manufacturers that distribute benchtop synthesis equipment;
- non-traditional provider settings named by the framework; and
- third-party vendors/intermediaries.

## Six control families

| Family | High-level governance objective | Evidence boundary |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Attestation | Provider/manufacturer makes an accountable adherence statement available through the stated channel | Self-attestation is not independent assurance |
| Order screening | Covered orders are checked for the framework's concern category | Operational thresholds and matching logic are deliberately omitted |
| Customer/order legitimacy | Relevant customer and intended-use context receive proportionate review | Detailed KYC checklist and examples are not reproduced |
| Reporting | Potentially illegitimate orders are not treated as ordinary fulfilment and follow the stated escalation expectation | Contact details and operational reporting routes are omitted |
| Record retention | Screening, review and response decisions retain auditable context | Retention alone does not establish accuracy or lawful use |
| Cybersecurity/information security | Protect screening-related and customer/proprietary information and consider secure design/supply dependencies | No configuration, deployment or test evidence is supplied |

## Requirements, recommendations and exceptions

The framework uses different modalities that should not be collapsed:

- agencies `will`, as appropriate and consistent with law, establish funding
  requirements under their authorities;
- providers/manufacturers `should` perform the actions needed to adhere;
- broader use outside the funding boundary is encouraged;
- referenced standards and future technical/conformity work were expected to
  refine implementation;
- rare case-specific exceptions could be issued by agency heads for stated
  health or national-security priorities through the framework's consultation
  process.

> **Claim record — GOV-0002-C04 | source-report**

## Implementation and assurance

The artifact discusses future technical standards, updates, possible conformity
assessment and exploration of independent audits. These are assurance designs,
not completed results. It provides no agency adoption table, provider/manufacturer
implementation record, coverage rate, screening-quality metric, false-positive/
false-negative analysis, incident outcome or independent audit.

## Framework relationships

The artifact says it implements direction from EO 14110, incorporates and
supplements portions of the 2023 HHS guidance, and anticipates further NIST
technical/conformity work. EO 14292 is now separately represented by
[GOV-0004](gov-0004-us-eo-14292-biological-research-2025.md) and
affects the status interpretation without rewriting the 2024 text. The other
documents are not treated as independent evidence or current crosswalks until
separately ingested.

`AST-0004`, `WFL-0008`, `SYS-0006` and `CTL-0006` use narrowly scoped
`governed-by` edges to this page. Each edge remains conditional on the funding,
actor, purchase and temporal boundaries above; it is not a conclusion that the
2024 framework currently governs every represented system or transaction.

## Safety and privacy boundary

This governance page deliberately withholds exact sequence windows and matching
logic, illustrative biological examples, detailed customer-verification fields,
reporting contact detail and equipment tamper/bypass mechanics. It retains the
defensive control functions, accountability relationships and privacy/security
objectives needed for governance analysis.
