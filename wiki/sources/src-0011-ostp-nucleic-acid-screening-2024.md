# Framework for Nucleic Acid Synthesis Screening, revised September 2024

## Bibliographic identity

National Science and Technology Council, Fast Track Action Committee on
Synthetic Nucleic Acid Procurement Screening. *Framework for Nucleic Acid
Synthesis Screening*. Revised September 2024; cover also identifies Version 1
as April 2024. The document describes itself as a product of an interagency
committee established to implement the nucleic-acid-screening direction in
Executive Order 14110.

This is an official US executive-branch framework and historical normative
source. It describes a federal-funding-condition design and recommended
provider/manufacturer practices; it is not itself a statute, regulation,
reported agency award term, conformity assessment or effectiveness study.

## Executive summary

As issued, the revised framework told federal life-sciences funding agencies to
establish, as appropriate and consistent with applicable law, procurement
conditions through their own award/contract authorities. It stated that the
requirement would apply to relevant purchases made on or after 26 April 2025,
while encouraging broader voluntary use.

The framework covers providers of synthetic nucleic acids, manufacturers of
benchtop synthesis equipment, federally funded customers and intermediaries.
It groups adherence into six control families: attestation; screening of
relevant orders; legitimacy review for relevant customers/orders; reporting of
potentially illegitimate orders; record retention; and cybersecurity/
information security. Rare case-specific agency exceptions were contemplated.

No implementation record, agency-term sample, adoption measure, screening
performance metric, audit finding, incident outcome or independent evaluation
appears in the artifact. Current status after later executive action is not
resolved by this source transaction.

## Scope and methods

This is a prescriptive framework, not a study. Its unit of governance is a
covered procurement relationship involving a federal life-sciences funding
condition, a customer/recipient, a provider or equipment manufacturer and, if
present, an intermediary. The instrument separates agency funding requirements
from provider/manufacturer adherence criteria.

The document reports no sampling, deployment survey, test protocol,
conformity-assessment result or outcome evaluation. References to future NIST
standards, periodic updates and possible independent audits are planned
assurance mechanisms, not completed evidence.

## Limitations and conflicts

- The framework is dated September 2024 and derives its direction from EO 14110.
  SRC-0013 records EO 14292's revise-or-replace direction. SRC-0060/GOV-0028
  later add one bounded current HHS award-policy implementation excluding NIH,
  while the completed successor and wider agency/award states remain
  unresolved.
- “Will require” and the stated 2025 purchase date describe the framework's
  as-issued design. Actual duties depend on applicable agency authority and
  award/contract terms not included here.
- The source does not establish adoption outside federally funded procurement;
  broader use is encouraged, not silently converted into a universal duty.
- Six control families are recommendations/conditions, not evidence of
  implementation, testing, screening accuracy, incident prevention or
  independent effectiveness.
- The framework incorporates HHS guidance and anticipates NIST work, but those
  separate documents are not independent lineages until directly ingested.
- Self-attestation, future conformity assessment and future audits are distinct
  assurance levels; none is an effectiveness result in this artifact.
- The issuer is the same executive branch that designed the framework. The
  document contains no external evaluation or affected-party analysis.

## Safety abstraction

The source was fully reviewed, but derivative pages intentionally omit exact
sequence thresholds/windows and matching logic, illustrative sequence/agent
examples, customer-verification checklist fields, reporting contact details,
and equipment tamper/bypass detection mechanics. Those details are not needed
to explain governance and could increase screening-evasion or misuse value when
consolidated.

## Version status

The cover says `Revised: September 2024` and `Version 1: April 2024`. Section VI
says the framework would be updated as technologies and screening mechanisms
advance and mentions exploration of independent audits. The artifact contains
no correction history or successor document.

The archived copy remains reproducible and byte-identical to the local file.
The current White House path's 404 response does not resolve policy status.
EO 14292 is now separately reconciled in `GOV-0002` and `SYN-0002`; any
completed revised/replacement framework and actual agency award/contract terms
still require separate transactions.
