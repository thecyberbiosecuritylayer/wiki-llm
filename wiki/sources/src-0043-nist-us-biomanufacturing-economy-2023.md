## Publication role and definition boundary

The landing page identifies Douglas Thomas, publication date 2023-06-23,
report number `100-52` and the NIST Advanced Manufacturing Series. The report
uses a broad biomass-production/conversion boundary for biomanufacturing and a
narrower growing/altering-biological-material boundary for advanced
biomanufacturing. These are analytical definitions for this economic report,
not law, regulation, a cybersecurity standard or universal terminology.

> **Claim record — SRC-0043-C02 | source-report**
> **Claim:** NIST AMS 100-52 explicitly distinguishes broad
> biomanufacturing from narrower advanced biomanufacturing and identifies the
> report's publication, author and technical-series status.
> **Claim status:** active
> **Scope:** Report-specific economic classification; not a binding sector
> definition, facility inventory, cyber architecture or adoption census.
> **As of:** Published 2023-06-23; official page reviewed 2026-07-12.
> **Review after:** 2027-01-12.
> **Evidence status:** single-source
> **Confidence:** high
> **Evidence:** Landing identity/abstract/citation; PDF title and abstract,
> physical pp. 1-4; report pp. 23-24/physical pp. 29-30.
> **Basis / limits:** Identity and classification are direct. The report
> itself defines the boundary under which its estimates are calculated.

## Industrial-biomanufacturing scope

Report page 23 lists food, beverage, tobacco, textile/apparel, leather, wood,
paper, chemical, pharmaceutical, rubber/plastic and furniture manufacturing
within the broad boundary. Pages 25-28 explain food/beverage, pharmaceutical,
bioplastics, biofuel/biochemical and predominantly mechanical industrial
branches. Table 5.1 preserves individual included proportions rather than
treating every industry as wholly advanced biomanufacturing.

> **Claim record — SRC-0043-C03 | source-report**

> rubber/plastic and furniture manufacturing.

## Supply-chain and estimate limits

The report separates direct production from equipment and other upstream
inputs counted through indirect value added. Its values are economic
estimates based on specified BEA, ASM and Manufacturing Cost Guide inputs;
some detailed relationships rely on 2012 data and selected industries are
only partially included.

> **Claim status:** active
> **Scope:** Economic method and boundary; not a digital supply-chain map,
