## One Health scope and explicit limits

The guide adopts the OHHLEP human–animal–plant–environment definition. It
expressly includes domestic and wild animals, terrestrial and aquatic animals,
plants, ecosystems, food, water and environmental drivers. Its target audience
is national authorities and governmental/nongovernmental collaborators across
these sectors. It is country-adaptable programme guidance, does not focus on a
particular technical area and does not replace ongoing national mechanisms.

> **Claim record — SRC-0021-C03 | source-report**
> **Claim:** The guide's One Health scope spans humans, domestic/wild and
> terrestrial/aquatic animals, plants and the wider environment/ecosystems,

## Three pathways and five-step programme lifecycle

The three theory-of-change pathways cover governance/policy/law/finance,
organizational development/implementation/sector integration, and data/
evidence/information systems/knowledge exchange. Five steps run from situation
analysis through coordination, planning, implementation and review/learning.
This is a national programme lifecycle, not breeding→production→movement→
trade→disposition or any other physical product lifecycle.

## Actors, sectors and coordination responsibility

National authorities hold primary programme/financial/resource responsibility;
the Quadripartite may support countries on request and subject to resources.
The guide recommends a multisectoral mechanism, secretariat, technical groups,
budget and role allocation. Figure 7 names human/animal health, environment,
food safety, plants, agriculture/aquaculture and wildlife, plus local government,
industry/private sector, education, academia/research, finance, justice,
defence/security and disaster management. Communities and Indigenous peoples
appear in local implementation. These are actor/sector classes, not an observed
country RACI or evidence of equitable participation.

plans and financing. It says national legal mechanisms may need to support
routine/emergency collaboration, data sharing, integrated surveillance and

## Information systems and knowledge exchange

Pathway 3 recommends integrated national information and early-warning systems
that capture, manage, analyse, predict, forecast and report surveillance data,
along with cross-sector data sharing and joint platforms/databases. It mentions
AI/big-data use and food-production/trade and environmental data. It does not
publish a farm OT, laboratory, remote-sensing, traceability or security
architecture, nor validate a trust boundary.

## Integration boundary

`SRC-0021` is one joint Quadripartite lineage, not four independent sources.
Its recommendations do not demonstrate national uptake. Its global scope does
not create a second national jurisdiction, and renewed cooperation does not
turn guidance into law. No new `AST/WFL/SYS/CTL` page is created because the
document supplies programme design classes rather than a concrete sector
architecture, product workflow or exact-edge implemented control.
