## One Health scope and zoonoses-centered boundary

The framework uses a human–domestic/wild-animal–plant–environment definition
and explicitly includes terrestrial/aquatic ecosystems. It names zoonoses,
emerging disease, antimicrobial resistance, food safety/security, vector-borne
disease, animal welfare, wildlife, invasive species, biodiversity/habitat and
land-use issues. Its operational programme is nonetheless primarily focused on
zoonotic disease and public-health preparedness; other One Health issues are
not specifically addressed.

> **Claim record — SRC-0020-C03 | source-report**
> **Claim:** NOHF-Zoonoses directly supports a broad One Health scope spanning
> people, domestic/wild animals, plants and shared terrestrial/aquatic
> environments, but its stated primary programme focus is zoonotic disease and
> public-health preparedness rather than every agricultural/environmental issue.
> **Claim status:** active
> **Scope:** U.S. framework scope/terms, not a complete global One Health or
> agriculture-sector taxonomy.
> **As of:** 2025-01-10.
> **Review after:** 2027-01-08.
> **Evidence status:** single-source
> **Confidence:** high
> **Evidence:** Executive Summary, PDF p. 3; Introduction, pp. 5–6; `Framework
> Purpose and Scope`, p. 7; Conclusion, p. 13.
> **Basis / limits:** Included domains and the primary-focus limit are direct.
> A broad definition does not establish complete assets/workflows/systems.

## Actors and coordinating structure

The U.S. One Health Coordination Unit was launched in January 2024 with shared
CDC/DOI/USDA leadership and a rotating chair. The framework connects federal
partners to state, Tribal, local and territorial government, NGOs, academia,
private-sector partners, affected communities, the public and international
partners. It calls for culturally appropriate engagement and appropriate use
of Indigenous knowledge. These are actor classes and collaboration goals, not
rights, observed participation quality or a complete agricultural RACI.

> **Claim record — SRC-0020-C04 | source-report**
> **Claim:** NOHF-Zoonoses establishes a federal OHCU coordination model with
> CDC/DOI/USDA shared leadership and names governmental, Tribal, academic,
> nongovernmental, private-sector, affected-community and public partners.
> **Claim status:** active
> **Scope:** Framework actor/governance design; not legal duties, rights,
> organization-specific responsibility or observed engagement.
> **As of:** 2026-07-12.
> **Review after:** 2026-10-10.
> **Evidence status:** single-source
> **Confidence:** high
> **Evidence:** Executive Summary, PDF pp. 3–4; Introduction, pp. 5–6;

> The framework does not allocate every farm/supply-chain operational role.

## Seven programme goals and lifecycle limit

The seven goals are coordination/communication, prevention, preparedness,
outbreak investigation/response/recovery, surveillance, laboratory and
workforce. This provides an emergency/public-health programme lifecycle, but
not a complete agriculture lifecycle from breeding/input and production through
movement/trade/traceability and disposition.

> **Claim record — SRC-0020-C05 | analytic-judgment**
> **Claim:** The framework covers coordination→prevention→preparedness→
> investigation/response/recovery→surveillance/laboratory/workforce functions,
> but does not provide a complete agricultural production/product lifecycle.
> **Claim status:** active
> **Scope:** Seven framework goals and observed lifecycle gap; not an assertion

> **Review after:** 2027-01-08.
> **Evidence status:** inferred

## Surveillance, information and model objectives

The framework calls for coordinated surveillance evaluation, secure information
sharing, timely exchange/interoperability and models/risk-prediction tools using
human, wildlife, domestic-animal, terrestrial/aquatic-ecosystem and geospatial
land/air/water/weather inputs. These are future objectives, not a deployed data
architecture, database inventory or validated prediction result.

> **Claim record — SRC-0020-C06 | source-report**
> **Claim:** NOHF-Zoonoses specifies cross-sector surveillance, secure sharing,
> interoperability and multi-domain risk-model inputs as objectives, without
> demonstrating a deployed architecture, model validation or decision outcome.
> **Claim status:** active
> **Scope:** Objectives 2.1, 3.5 and Goal 5; not system topology, dataset access,
> a current model catalogue or effectiveness evidence.
> **As of:** 2025-01-10.
> **Review after:** 2027-01-08.

validated assay development, capacity gaps, reporting/timely data sharing,

capacity/control effectiveness.

## Exercise and assurance design

The preparedness goal calls for training/interagency exercises, review and
implementation of completed-exercise recommendations. Goal 4 calls for
after-action review of response/recovery plans. These are assurance designs;
the framework contains no particular exercise, result, metric, implemented
recommendation or independent evaluation.
