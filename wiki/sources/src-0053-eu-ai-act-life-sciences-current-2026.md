# EU AI Act, GPAI and medical-device applicability package with pending 2026 Omnibus status

## Identity, acquisition and complete review

The retained package contains six official objects: the complete 144-page
English Official Journal text of Regulation (EU) 2024/1689; current Commission
pages for the GPAI guidelines and Code of Practice; the complete 27-page joint
Artificial Intelligence Board and Medical Device Coordination Group FAQ; the
European Parliament first-reading text adopted on 16 June 2026; and the
European Parliament Legislative Observatory procedure record checked on
12 July 2026. The six objects total 3,553,179 bytes and match every declared
frontmatter hash.

Both complete PDFs, comprising 171 pages, were text-extracted and structurally
inspected; the cited legal/FAQ pages and the substantive identity, force,
application, exception, medical-device, GPAI and procedure-status portions of
the HTML components were reviewed. Both PDFs are tagged, unencrypted and
unsigned, with no form, JavaScript or embedded file. Text extraction was
coherent. `qpdf` was

## Base Act force and staged application

Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 was published on 12 July 2024 and entered into force
on the twentieth following day, 1 August 2024. Article 113 makes the Regulation
binding in its entirety and directly applicable, while separating entry into
force from application: Chapters I and II apply from 2 February 2025; Chapter
III Section 4, Chapter V, Chapter VII, Chapter XII and Article 78 apply from
2 August 2025 subject to the stated Article 101 exception; the general date is
2 August 2026; and Article 6(1) plus corresponding product-system obligations
apply from 2 August 2027 under the unamended text.

## Territorial, research, data-law and open-source boundaries

Article 2(1) covers providers placing AI systems or GPAI models on the Union
market, Union deployers, specified third-country providers/deployers where the
output is used in the Union, importers, distributors, product manufacturers,
authorised representatives and affected persons. Article 2(6) excludes systems,
models and outputs specifically developed and put into service for the sole
purpose of scientific R&D. Article 2(8) separately excludes pre-market research,
testing and development activity, but not testing in real-world conditions and
not other applicable Union law. Article 2(7) preserves data-protection, privacy
and communications-confidentiality law. Article 2(12)'s free/open-source system
exclusion does not cover systems placed on the market or put into service as
high-risk systems or systems under Articles 5 or 50.

> **Claim record — SRC-0053-C03 | source-report**
> **Claim:** The AI Act does not create a blanket research or open-source
> exemption: sole-purpose scientific R&D and pre-market activity are bounded
> exclusions, real-world testing remains outside the latter, and applicable
> data/privacy law remains in place.
> **Claim status:** active
> **Scope:** Article 2 applicability boundaries; not classification of a named
> laboratory, model, dataset, clinical study or deployment.
> **As of:** Base Act reviewed 2026-07-12.
> **Review after:** 2026-07-19.
> **Evidence status:** single-source
> **Confidence:** high
> **Evidence:** AI Act Article 2(1), (6)-(8) and (12), Official Journal PDF
> physical pp. 45-46.
> **Basis / limits:** The conditions and retained-law boundary are direct;
> fact-specific intended purpose, market and real-world-testing questions remain.

## Medical-device and IVD trigger

Article 6(1) classifies product-related AI as high-risk only when both limbs are
met: the AI is a safety component or itself a product under Annex I legislation,
and the product or AI requires third-party conformity assessment. Annex I,
Section A, points 11 and 12 name the MDR and IVDR. Article 43(3) directs such
systems through the relevant sectoral conformity procedure and incorporates
AI Act Chapter III Section 2 requirements into that assessment.

The nonbinding June 2025 FAQ applies the two-limb test in Q2/Table 1, explains
in Q4 that AI high-risk status does not reclassify a device under MDR/IVDR,
treats clinical investigations/performance studies as real-world testing in
Q25, and addresses integrated conformity assessment in Q27-Q28. Q35 says an
in-house device satisfying MDR/IVDR Article 5(5) is not normally subject to
third-party assessment and therefore does not meet Article 6(1) on that basis;
other AI Act provisions can still apply. Its first page expressly says that it
is not a Commission document, is not legally binding and cannot replace CJEU
interpretation.

> **Claim record — SRC-0053-C04 | source-report**
> **Claim:** MDR/IVDR AI is not automatically high-risk: Article 6(1) requires
> both a product/safety-component role and third-party conformity assessment,
> after which Article 43(3) integrates AI requirements into the sectoral route.
> **Claim status:** active
> **Scope:** EU product-law trigger and nonbinding interpretive examples; not
> classification, conformity, approval or safety of a named device or IVD.

## High-risk requirements and assurance boundary

For systems to which Chapter III Section 2 applies, Articles 8-15 cover
compliance integration, risk management, data/data-governance, technical
documentation, logging, transparency, human oversight, accuracy, robustness
and cybersecurity. These are normative design and lifecycle duties. A required
test, document, log or conformity route is not a reported implementation or
successful control result.

## GPAI obligations, exceptions and current presentation

Article 51 creates systemic-risk classification, including the stated
10^25-FLOP training-compute presumption and Commission designation route.
Article 53 requires model and downstream documentation, a Union-copyright
policy and a public training-content summary. Its open-source exception covers
only paragraph 1(a)-(b) and does not apply to systemic-risk models; copyright
and training-summary duties are not removed by that exception. Article 55 adds
evaluation/adversarial testing, systemic-risk assessment and mitigation,
serious-incident reporting and cybersecurity duties for systemic-risk models.

The Commission page, last updated 28 April 2026, says GPAI provider obligations
entered application on 2 August 2025, Commission enforcement powers apply from
2 August 2026 and pre-2-August-2025 models must comply by 2 August 2027. The
same page expressly calls its guidelines nonbinding Commission interpretation.
The Code page, last updated 23 April 2026, calls the Code voluntary, dates its
publication to 10 July 2025 and records Commission/AI Board assessment as an
adequate voluntary compliance tool. Neither page turns the Code into law or
establishes provider compliance.

## Adopted amendment awaiting Official Journal publication

The Parliament first-reading position of 16 June 2026 would replace Article
113(c) so that Chapter III Sections 1-3, except Article 6(5), apply from
2 December 2027 for Article 6(2)/Annex III high-risk systems and from
2 August 2028 for Article 6(1)/Annex I product systems. Its Article 4 provides
entry into force on the third day following Official Journal publication.

The OEIL record says Parliament decided at first reading on 16 June 2026 and
the Council adopted the act after that first reading on 29 June 2026. On
12 July 2026 its displayed status remained `Procedure completed, awaiting
publication in Official Journal`. Therefore the adopted dates describe the
pending amending text, not an amendment already in force. Until publication
and the text's entry-into-force clause operate, the base Article 113 dates in
C02 remain the current legal dates.
