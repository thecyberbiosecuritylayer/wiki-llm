## Programme and evaluation boundary

PATH-SAFE was a 2021–2025, four-nation UK One Health programme for coordinated
surveillance of foodborne pathogens/disease and antimicrobial resistance
across agriculture, food and the environment. The final evaluation covers the
March 2024–March 2025 funding year and builds on RAND's Phase-1 evaluation.
FSA led the programme with FSS, Defra, APHA, Cefas, EA, VMD, DHSC and UKHSA as
core partners and more than 65 public, academic and private delivery partners.

The theory-based contribution evaluation used a programme-developed logic
model/outcome framework, searches and document review, an online survey, five
oversight interviews and two case studies with five additional interviews.
About 154 people were invited to the survey and 33 completed it (about 21%)
between 7 and 29 November 2024. Internal documents available by 10 March 2025
were extracted against evaluation criteria; many closure reports and outputs
were unavailable because programme delivery and evaluation ended together.

The report visibly duplicates a citations paragraph on p. 20 and a heading on
p. 22; p. 36 duplicates a wastewater-analysis bullet and pp. 50–51 contain
duplicate bibliography records. They are production defects, not repeated
evidence. Funding figures on p. 8 omit a currency symbol in the PDF and are
not silently relabelled here.

The evaluation says 33 people completed the survey, but its displayed
delivery (`n=14`) and external (`n=18`) subgroups total 32; the missing unit is
unexplained. Figure 4's narrative says 67% expected scientific-data sharing,
while the chart shows 8 Agree, 4 neutral, 1 disagree and 1 do-not-know out of
14, or 57% Agree and zero Strongly agree. Neither form is silently repaired.
Annex B is the questionnaire, not response data, and Annex C is an interview
guide, not transcripts. Annex A's AMR overview says five projects but lists
four bullets before later §A.4.5, and the annex closes with placeholder
`Project reference: FSXXXXXX`.

Date presentations also remain artifact-specific. The DOI record gives
2025-06-18; the retained GOV.UK evaluation page calls the final report
published 2025-06-23. The APHA report is dated July 2024, while its PDF build
metadata and Defra landing date are 2024-11-08. These are not normalized into
one unqualified date.

## Assets, settings and functional workflow

The represented asset envelope includes novel, routine and archived animal,
food and environmental samples; isolates; sequence, phenotype/genotype,
metadata and analytic outputs; pathogen/AMR baselines; protocols, assays,
models and a genomic platform; data-sharing agreements; operational knowledge
and partner capability. Settings include bulk milk and livestock feed,
abattoirs and raw pet food, shellfish/coastal/river/wastewater environments,
hospitals and a care home, and official food-control contexts.

The programme therefore supplies a bounded, directly implemented functional
path: collection or reuse of a sample → transport/processing → qPCR,
sequencing, culture or other analysis → genomic/metadata integration and
source/pathway assessment → surveillance prioritisation, notification or
response decision → trial, scale-up or business-as-usual gate. It does not
define one universal procedure, complete material custody, treatment,
regulated movement, recall/disposition or farm-production lifecycle.

## Systems and data-sharing boundaries

The programme delivered a genomic data platform for Salmonella, *E. coli* and
*Listeria* analytics, AMR and associated metadata. It reports project-level
exchange among public agencies, laboratories and partners and deposits in or
connections to EnteroBase, ENA, SRA, NCBI and PubMLST. APHA, FSS and
UKHSA committed to trial the platform alongside existing surveillance, but
regular sharing, maintenance, expansion and business-as-usual ownership still
needed agreements and funding.

No programme-level sharing agreement existed. Bilateral negotiation,
different interpretations of legal/data-protection requirements, sensitive
health/genomic data, inconsistent metadata, linkage/anonymisation questions
and unavailable private veterinary/water-company datasets remained material
boundaries. The source supplies system/component and organizational-boundary
evidence, but no validated platform topology, IAM design, security-interface
test or complete veterinary/plant-laboratory architecture.

## Bulk-milk implementation and primary null survey result

PATH-SAFE's earlier bulk-milk work produced feasibility evidence, milk-sector
relationships and APHA sample-handling, transport and laboratory-processing
workflows. The evaluation says the pilot sampled about 26% of the total Great
Britain dairy-cattle population. Those programme relationships, infrastructure
and workflow evidence supported a Defra discussion/proposal and the Animal
Disease Policy Group's selection of a separate urgent H5N1 bulk-milk survey
after the March 2024 U.S. dairy-cattle detections.

The records do not use one stable milk-organization name. Main-report p. 24
prints `National Milk Laboratories`/`NMLs`, while p. 25 prints `National Milk
Records`; the primary APHA report identifies the 2024-survey counterparty as
the `National Milk Records (NMR) company`. Those artifact-specific forms are
preserved rather than normalized to one verified legal entity.

The separately retained APHA primary report is more precise about the new
survey. From 23 May to 27 June 2024, APHA tested 508 bulk-milk samples
representing 455 farms across England, Scotland and Wales, supplied through
voluntarily participating processors. Its plan
targeted a 99% probability of detecting at least one infected farm if national
between-herd prevalence were at least 1%; the minimum calculated sample was
453 farms from a frame of nearly 7,000. All samples were negative by the
reported HPAI H5N1 real-time RT-PCR tests.

The correct inference is time-, population- and design-bounded: disease was
not detected in the sample and, under the study assumptions, was absent or
below 1% herd-level prevalence during that period. Voluntary participation,
anonymisation/limited traceability and the six-week window constrain use. The
report's statements about specificity and spiked-sample performance are
author reports, not an independently retained validation dataset.

## Adoption, outcome and maturity evidence

PATH-SAFE reports many developed or validated methods and several bounded
practice changes, including an APHA change in processing hybrid short/long-read
WGS data. It also provides unusually useful maturity and limitation evidence:

- only 14% of delivery respondents (`n=14`) agreed that developed methods or
  tools had already led to specific surveillance improvements;
- 72% of external respondents (`n=18`) were uncertain whether methods were
  suitable for scale-up;
- only 38% of external respondents (`n=18`) and 50% of delivery respondents
  (`n=14`) agreed that PATH-SAFE improved data sharing;
- several projects were delayed or incomplete, few methods had entered
  business as usual, and platform ownership, funding, scale-up and comparative
  cost-effectiveness remained unresolved.

Relationship and proof-of-concept percentages measure small-sample participant
perceptions, not operational effect. The care home self-reported 24-hour
results, local cleaning/mask actions, reduced staff sickness in the affected
areas, corresponding agency-staff cost reduction and no major viral outbreak,
but those outcomes were unplanned, not systematically tracked, had no
baseline/comparator, preceded completion of broader analysis and were
explicitly left as a future hypothesis.

## Evaluator independence and current follow-up boundary

RAND Europe provides a distinguishable external evaluator role within an
FSA/RAND commissioned report, but the evidence base is largely internal
documentation and self-report, the logic framework was co-developed with
programme participants and the evaluation ended before long-term outcomes. It
could not establish a counterfactual or attribute national decisions solely to
PATH-SAFE. It recommends an independent 2027 follow-up to measure sustained
adoption, collaboration and outbreak detection/response outcomes.

The retained current GOV.UK record says PATH-SAFE ended in March 2025. The FSA
blog dated 2026-04-21 reports nine additional papers, citation/view metrics, a
claimed Netherlands policy use, continued 2025/26 monitoring/evaluation and a
launched successor Food Surveillance Programme that is integrating outputs.
It also says a technical synthesis was targeted for Summer 2026; completion
was not verified. The final evaluation's statement that another H5 survey was
likely in Spring 2025 is likewise prospective and unverified. These are
current issuer-reported follow-up signals. The cited papers, Dutch policy,
analytics datasets and successor-programme technical evidence were not
independently retained or verified; they do not retroactively satisfy the
2025 evaluation's missing effectiveness result.

## Frozen-rubric classification and safety boundary

PATH-SAFE materially strengthens `AGE-AS`, `AGE-WL`, `AGE-SD`, `AGE-XT` and
`AGE-CT`, but none meets its complete literal SF2/SF3 criterion. Seeds and
complete herd records remain incomplete; treatment, regulated movement and
disposition are missing; veterinary/plant-laboratory architecture, IAM and
validated trust boundaries are incomplete; the forward hostile cyber path,
cyber controls, explicit traceability-control boundary and agriculture-
specific trusted-recovery/resilience exact-edge mapping remain incomplete.

The package moves `AGE-CI` and `AGE-AE` from Absent to Partial. APHA supplies
the first retained primary empirical agriculture survey/result and RAND gives
bounded evaluation context, but the null finding is not a qualifying animal,
food, ecosystem or material-economic consequence with independent empirical
confirmation. Implemented pilots, metrics, operational use, a distinguishable
evaluator role and maturity/limitation evidence still do not give any complete
safeguard an appropriate test metric plus qualifying independent effectiveness
evaluation. The package creates no `INC` page and no new Pass.
