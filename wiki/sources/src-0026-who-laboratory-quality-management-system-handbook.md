## Scope and document modality

The handbook was developed collaboratively by WHO, CDC and CLSI and organizes
laboratory QMS material around CLSI's twelve Quality System Essentials. It is a
technical/training reference based on then-current ISO 15189:2007 and CLSI
GP26-A3/HS1-A2 material. Citations to older standards, CLIA descriptions or
accreditation processes are historical context, not current 2026 governance.

> **Claim record — SRC-0026-C02 | source-report**
> **Claim:** WHO defines `health laboratories` inclusively across clinical,
> diagnostic, medical, public-health, animal and environmental laboratories
> performing tests for diagnosis, screening, prevention, treatment decisions,
> surveillance or public-health purposes.
> **Claim status:** active
> **Scope:** The handbook's inclusive terminology; not a current legal
> classification or proof that every workflow applies identically to every lab.
> **As of:** 2011-01-01.
> **Review after:** 2026-10-10.
> **Evidence status:** single-source
> **Confidence:** high
> **Evidence:** Foreword and Quality System Essentials, pp. 7–8; inclusive
> `Health laboratories` note, p. 8; current WHO page Overview.
> **Basis / limits:** Named laboratory classes and purposes are literal. The
> source has no genomics-specific chapter and uses some class terms
> interchangeably within its own scope.

> **Claim record — SRC-0026-C03 | source-report**
> **Claim:** The handbook's QMS model coordinates organization, personnel,
> equipment, purchasing/inventory, process control, information, documents/
> records, occurrence management, assessment, improvement, customer service
> and facilities/safety across the laboratory system.
> **Claim status:** active
> **Scope:** WHO/CDC/CLSI technical design guidance, not binding law, a current
> ISO 15189 edition, accredited conformity or measured deployment.
> **As of:** 2011-01-01.
> **Review after:** 2026-10-10.
> **Evidence status:** single-source
> **Confidence:** high
> **Evidence:** Foreword, p. 7; §§1-2–1-5, pp. 11–17; rendered QMS model,
> pp. 13–15; chapter structure, pp. 19–222.
> **Basis / limits:** The twelve families and system framing are direct. WHO,
> CDC and CLSI collaboration creates one handbook lineage, not three
> independent sources.

> through collection, transport, laboratory analysis, report creation/
> transport and result interpretation, grouped into pre-examination,
> examination and post-examination phases.
> **Claim status:** active
> **Scope:** Functional clinical-laboratory testing path; not one product,
> protocol, deployed interface or public-health surveillance architecture.
> **As of:** 2011-01-01.
> **Review after:** 2026-10-10.
> **Evidence status:** single-source
> **Confidence:** high

## Safety handling

The page retains only defensive asset, lifecycle, quality-hazard, information-
system and control classes. It omits laboratory locations, patient/sample data,
collection/transport procedures, pathogen-specific settings, credentials,
interface details and misuse-enabling content.
