# Executive Order 14292 — Improving the Safety and Security of Biological Research

## Bibliographic identity

The President signed Executive Order 14292 on 5 May 2025. The Office of the
Federal Register published it on 8 May 2025 at 90 FR 19611–19614 as Federal
Register document `2025-08266`. This transaction uses the official GPO/
GovInfo PDF as the immutable primary artifact.

This is a presidential directive to executive-branch officials, not the
revised/replacement nucleic-acid-screening framework that it orders OSTP to
produce. It must also be distinguished from agency award terms, provider
implementation, conformity testing and effectiveness evidence.

## Screening-relevant directions

Section 4(b) gives OSTP, coordinating with the named White House and agency
officials, a 90-day direction to revise or replace the 2024 *Framework for
Nucleic Acid Synthesis Screening*. It directs life-science-funding agencies to
ensure covered procurement uses providers or manufacturers adhering to the
updated framework, requires the updated framework to incorporate §7
enforcement mechanisms, and calls for review/revision at least every four
years or as appropriate.

> **Claim record — SRC-0013-C02 | source-report**
> **Claim:** Section 4(b) directs OSTP to revise or replace the 2024 screening
> framework within 90 days and directs life-science-funding agencies to connect
> covered procurement to provider/manufacturer adherence under the updated
> framework, including its future enforcement mechanism and periodic review.
> **Claim status:** active
> **Scope:** Direction stated in the order; not proof of publication,
> implementation, uniform agency terms, provider adherence or effectiveness.
> **As of:** 2025-05-05.
> **Review after:** 2026-08-11.
> **Evidence status:** single-source

Section 5 separately directs development and implementation of a strategy for
specified non-federally funded research. The strategy is to include action
toward comprehensive, scalable and verifiable nucleic-acid synthesis screening
in non-federal settings and a legislative proposal for any necessary authority
gaps.

## Future funding terms and legal boundaries

Section 7 directs HHS and other relevant agencies, consistently with existing
law and regulation, to include future terms in life-science research grants
and contracts. At safe abstraction, these terms address material compliance,
recipient certification, institutional responsibility and possible funding
revocation or time-bounded ineligibility. They are policy enforcement design,
not a sample award clause or an adjudicated enforcement event.

## Current-status evidence boundary

The order changes what can safely be said about the 2024 framework: a later
presidential directive required it to be revised or replaced and tied the
future version to procurement and enforcement. The order does **not** say that
its own four pages are the updated framework, identify a completed successor,
or report adoption, testing, audit or effectiveness results.

## Scope and methods

This is an official legal/policy text, not a study. The unit of governance is
executive-branch direction to OSTP and relevant agencies, subject to the
order's legal and appropriation boundaries. For this transaction, the order is
used only to reconcile nucleic-acid screening governance and funding-term
design; its broader biological-research policy is not generalized into a
complete research-oversight model.

## Limitations and conflicts

- `revise or replace` is an ordered future action. It is not silently converted
  into `rescinded`, `superseded`, `completed` or `implemented`.
- Deadline passage is not completion evidence. A successor artifact or agency
  implementation record must be independently acquired and reviewed.
- Section 7 specifies a future enforcement design; actual grant and contract
  terms, legal interpretations and enforcement cases are separate evidence.
  SRC-0060 later supplies one bounded current HHS award-policy mechanism, but
  it does not identify the completed successor or prove award-level compliance.
- Search results revealed secondary descriptions that call the 2024 framework
  rescinded. This page does not adopt that stronger term because the primary
  order itself says `revise or replace` and does not identify a completed
  replacement.
- The order provides no adoption rate, screening benchmark, control test,
  audit result, incident outcome or causal-effectiveness evidence.

## Safety boundary

The page preserves governance actors, modalities, deadlines and assurance
boundaries. It omits the order's detailed biological outcome list, harmful
research examples, sequence-screening parameters, evasion logic and
operational reporting contacts. No facility, target, protocol or bypass detail
is needed for the policy finding.
