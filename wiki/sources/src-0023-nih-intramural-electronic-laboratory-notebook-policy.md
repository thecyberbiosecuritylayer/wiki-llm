## Scope, modality and textual ambiguity

The title and introduction call the assembled material the NIH IRP ELN Policy
and state that IRP investigators are required to use electronic resources for
new and ongoing research as of 2024-06-30. Sections III.A–B define NIH IRP
Notebook Owners and Users, but twice call the same page `draft guidance`.
The current companion says NIH intramural staff are expected to follow NIH
policy and that non-adherence may lead to disciplinary action. This package is
therefore retained as an internal institutional policy/guidance instrument with
an explicit textual modality inconsistency—not a statute, regulation,
accreditation standard, general extramural-grant rule or universal laboratory
requirement.

## Literal ELN definition and record function

> **Claim record — SRC-0023-C03 | source-report**
> **Claim:** NIH defines an ELN as a system that creates, stores, retrieves and
> shares electronic research records, replaces paper-notebook functions and
> serves as the complete reproducible research record linking rationale,
> methods, samples, results, data location, analysis and interpretation.
> **Claim status:** active
> **Scope:** NIH IRP functional definition; not a universal ELN definition,
> deployed product architecture or validation finding.
> **As of:** 2026-07-12.
> **Review after:** 2026-08-11.
> **Evidence status:** single-source
> **Confidence:** high
> **Evidence:** Local HTML, §II; companion `Scientific Record Keeping —
> Electronic Notebooks`, printed p. 9/physical p. 12.
> **Basis / limits:** The function is direct in both manifestations of one NIH
> institutional lineage; stated reproducibility purpose is not a measured
> reproducibility outcome.

> **Claim record — SRC-0023-C04 | source-report**
> **Claim:** The policy/companion place human-entered records, program output,
> imaging and direct scientific-instrument output inside the ELN record
> boundary, while paper-only benchtop-instrument output is captured as an
> electronic record.
> **Claim status:** active
> **Scope:** Class-level instrument/output↔record association; not a live
> interface, command path, automated transfer, calibration result or data-
> integrity test.
> **As of:** 2026-07-12.
> **Review after:** 2026-08-11.
> **Evidence status:** single-source
> **Confidence:** high
> **Evidence:** Local HTML, §I final bullet and §IV.A opening; companion
> `Scientific Record Keeping — Electronic Notebooks`, printed p. 9/physical
> p. 12.
> **Basis / limits:** Input classes and association are literal. No actual
> instrument/ELN deployment is observed.

## Identity, custody, integrity and storage boundaries

> **Claim record — SRC-0023-C05 | source-report**
> **Claim:** NIH assigns research-record ownership to NIH, ELN control/
> availability to the responsible investigator, institutional access/control
> authority to NIH/IC leadership and user authorization to named roles,
> creating explicit user↔PI↔institution↔record custody boundaries.
> **Claim status:** active
> **Scope:** Institutional responsibility and authorization design; not an
> observed authentication decision, least-privilege test or ownership rule
> outside NIH IRP.
> **As of:** 2026-07-12.
> **Review after:** 2026-08-11.
> **Evidence status:** single-source
> **Confidence:** high
> **Evidence:** Local HTML, §§III.A–B, IV.C and VI.A; companion `Scientific
> Record Keeping — Electronic Notebooks`, printed pp. 8–9/physical pp. 11–12.
> **Basis / limits:** Actors and intended authorization/custody edges are
> direct. The source reports requirements, not access logs or test results.

> off-site backup and resistance to later alteration.
> **Claim status:** active
> **Scope:** Records-management and integrity/availability design; not proof
> that a specific platform implements, tests or effectively operates each
> function.
> **As of:** 2026-07-12.
> **Review after:** 2026-08-11.

> **Evidence:** Local HTML, §VI.A; companion `Scientific Record Keeping —

> Storage of Research Records`, printed pp. 12–13/physical pp. 15–16.
> **Basis / limits:** Required functions are literal. Requirement and source-
> asserted capability are not implementation, completed validation or outcome.

## Safety handling

The wiki retains only defensive class-level record, identity, integrity,
storage and authorization boundaries. It omits account-request paths, named-
platform configurations, internal dashboards, storage addresses, credentials,
operational monitoring detail and any research/material record.
