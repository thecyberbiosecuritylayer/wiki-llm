# Regulation (EU) 2025/327 on the European Health Data Space

## Bibliographic identity and legal status

The European Parliament and Council signed Regulation (EU) 2025/327 on
11 February 2025. It was published in the Official Journal on 5 March 2025,
entered into force on the twentieth following day—25 March 2025—and is binding
in its entirety and directly applicable in all Member States. Most provisions
do not yet apply: general application begins 26 March 2027 and Articles 105
stages major primary-use, EHR-system, secondary-use and omics provisions through
2029, 2031 and 2035.

The Commission's DG SANTE overview says `26 March 2025` for entry into force.
That conflicts with Article 105 and EUR-Lex metadata, which both yield
25 March 2025. This page uses the primary legal text and records the overview
error rather than silently harmonising it.

## Scope, electronic health data and use modalities

EHDS distinguishes personal from non-personal electronic health data and
primary from secondary use. It complements other Union acts rather than
replacing GDPR, Data Governance Act, Data Act, clinical-trial, trade-secret,
medical-device, IVD or AI rules.

> **Claim record — SRC-0016-C03 | source-report**
> **Claim:** EHDS establishes common rules, standards, infrastructures and
> governance for personal/non-personal electronic health data across primary
> healthcare use and separately governed secondary use, while preserving the
> stated roles of other Union legal acts.
> **Claim status:** active
> **Scope:** Regulation-specific scope and legal interaction, not a complete
> statement of every EU or national health-data obligation.
> **As of:** 2026-07-12.
> **Review after:** 2027-01-08.
> **Evidence status:** single-source
> **Confidence:** high
> **Evidence:** Articles 1(1)–(9) and 2(1)(a), 2(2)(a)–(f), PDF pp. 29–33.
> **Basis / limits:** Use modalities, data classes and non-displacement clauses
> are explicit. Underlying referenced acts are not independently restated here.

## Human omics and other secondary-use categories

Article 51's minimum secondary-use categories include human genetic,
epigenomic and genomic data; proteomic, transcriptomic, metabolomic, lipidomic
and other human omic data; pathogen data; clinical studies; research cohorts;
and biobank data/associated databases. Member States may add safeguards for
specified sensitive classes. Article 105 defers several research and omics
categories to 26 March 2031.

> **Claim record — SRC-0016-C04 | source-report**
> **Claim:** EHDS defines a broad minimum secondary-use envelope that includes
> multiple human-omics, pathogen, clinical-study, cohort and biobank data
> classes, permits stricter national safeguards for named sensitive classes
> and stages genetic/genomic/omics application to 26 March 2031.
> **Claim status:** active
> **Scope:** EU secondary-use categories and timing; not proof that a dataset
> is already available, interoperable, high quality or lawfully accessible.
> **As of:** 2026-07-12.
> **Review after:** 2027-01-08.
> **Evidence status:** single-source
> **Confidence:** high
> **Evidence:** Article 51(1)(d), (f)–(g), (m), (p)–(q) and 51(4), PDF
> pp. 57–58; Article 105, PDF p. 90.
> **Basis / limits:** Categories, Member State safeguard option and date are
> explicit. The legal list is not a universal scientific taxonomy or adoption
> record.

## Primary-use rights and EHR-system boundary

The Regulation specifies patient access, insertion, rectification, portability,
restriction and log-notification mechanisms; professional access and emergency
override boundaries; priority categories spanning summaries, prescriptions,
dispensations, imaging, laboratory/diagnostic results and discharge reports;
and common exchange-format and EHR-component rules. Annex I includes transfusion
medicine within laboratory/diagnostic results.

> **Claim record — SRC-0016-C05 | source-report**
> **Claim:** EHDS defines a staged primary-use/EHR envelope connecting patient
> and professional access rights, identity/provenance, priority clinical-data
> categories, cross-border exchange and harmonised interoperability/logging
> components.
> **Claim status:** active
> **Scope:** Normative EU design; not evidence that a Member State, provider or
> product has implemented or passed these requirements.
> **As of:** 2026-07-12.
> **Review after:** 2027-01-08.
> **Evidence status:** single-source
> **Confidence:** high
> **Evidence:** Articles 3–16, PDF pp. 34–39; Article 23, PDF pp. 42–43;
> Articles 25–30, PDF pp. 44–46; Annexes I–II, PDF pp. 92–94; Article 105,
> PDF pp. 90–91.
> **Basis / limits:** Rights, data categories, component functions and staging
> are direct. Implementing specifications and operational conformance are
> separate future/current evidence questions.

## Secondary-use purposes, prohibitions and opt-out

Secondary access is purpose- and permit-bounded. Permitted purposes include
public health, regulation, education, research and algorithm training/testing/
evaluation. Article 54 prohibits specified detrimental decisions,
discrimination, marketing and harmful product/service purposes. Article 71
creates a reversible opt-out for future permits, subject to a bounded national
public-interest exception with additional safeguards.

> **Claim record — SRC-0016-C06 | source-report**
> **Claim:** EHDS separates permitted secondary-use purposes from prohibited
> uses and creates a reversible prospective opt-out with a conditioned
> public-interest exception; a permitted purpose alone does not create access
> outside the application/permit/safeguard process.
> **Claim status:** active
> **Scope:** Chapter IV normative design, not a permit, processing event,
> population outcome or finding that a use is lawful under every other act.
> **As of:** 2026-07-12.
> **Review after:** 2027-01-08.
> **Evidence status:** single-source
> **Confidence:** high
> **Evidence:** Articles 53–54, PDF pp. 59–60; Articles 67–71, PDF pp. 69–74.
> **Basis / limits:** Purposes, prohibitions, permit path and opt-out boundaries
> are explicit. National-law conditions and other legal bases remain external.

## Secure processing and accountability safeguards

Secondary personal-data access is placed in secure processing environments
with named/unique users, access limitation, logging, monitoring, anonymous-only
download review, regular audits including third-party audits and corrective
action. Health data holders, access bodies and users receive differentiated
controller/processor roles.

> **Claim record — SRC-0016-C07 | source-report**
> **Claim:** EHDS requires permit-bounded secure processing environments,
> access/logging/monitoring controls, output restrictions, regular audit and
> corrective action for secondary use, with explicit holder/access-body/user
> responsibility allocation.
> **Claim status:** active
> **Scope:** Legal safeguard and accountability design; not a deployed secure
> environment, completed audit or effectiveness result.
> **As of:** 2026-07-12.
> **Review after:** 2027-01-08.
> **Evidence status:** single-source
> **Confidence:** high
> **Evidence:** Articles 57–61, PDF pp. 61–65; Articles 66–75, especially
> 73–75, PDF pp. 69–78.
> **Basis / limits:** Functions and roles are direct. Technical implementing
> specifications, deployments, audit results and measured outcomes are absent.

## EHR conformance, correction and incident design

EHDS requires interoperability and logging components for covered EHR systems,
manufacturer lifecycle documentation and self-declared conformity, digital
testing-environment assessment, market surveillance, non-conformity correction,
withdrawal/recall and serious-incident reporting. Annex III requires technical
documentation to include architecture, versions, lifecycle changes and
verification/validation test results.

> **Claim record — SRC-0016-C08 | source-report**
> **Claim:** EHDS creates staged EHR-system conformity, technical-documentation,
> testing-environment, market-surveillance, correction/withdrawal and serious-
> incident-reporting duties, but the Regulation supplies no product result or
> field-effectiveness evaluation.
> **Claim status:** active
> **Scope:** Regulatory design for covered EHR systems and claimed
> interoperability; not every healthcare software product or an observed case.
> **As of:** 2026-07-12.
> **Review after:** 2027-01-08.
> **Evidence status:** single-source
> **Confidence:** high
> **Evidence:** Articles 25–46, PDF pp. 44–55; Annexes II–IV, PDF pp. 93–96;
> Article 105, PDF pp. 90–91.
> **Basis / limits:** Duties and documentation scope are direct. A declaration,
> test requirement or surveillance power is not evidence of implementation or
> independent effectiveness.

## Governance architecture and transparency

The Regulation allocates responsibilities among Member States, digital health
authorities, health data access bodies, national contact points, the Commission,
the EHDS Board, health data holders/users and EHR economic operators. It creates
`MyHealth@EU` for primary use and `HealthData@EU` for secondary use, plus public
catalogues, biennial activity reporting, audit/penalty metrics and future
evaluations.

> and cross-border accountability dependencies with public reporting and
> future evaluation mechanisms across primary and secondary use.
> **Claim status:** active

> **As of:** 2026-07-12.
