# NIH genomic-data sharing and generative-AI controlled-access policy package, current through 2026 proposal status

## Identity, acquisition and complete review

The retained package contains the complete official NIH Guide response bodies
for the final 2014 Genomic Data Sharing Policy (`NOT-OD-14-124`), the 28 March
2025 generative-AI/controlled-human-genomic-data clarification
(`NOT-OD-25-081`) and the 17 December 2025 RFI containing a draft
Controlled-Access Data Policy and proposed GDS revisions (`NOT-OD-26-023`).
The three objects total 135,752 bytes and match the primary frontmatter hashes.
All substantive policy, purpose, scope, force, effective-date, consent,
exception, AI-model and proposal-status content was reviewed.

Direct command-line retrieval received a Cloudflare challenge, so the retained
objects are the actual official network response bodies delivered to a normal
browser after the challenge, not reconstructed text or a rendered DOM. A second
official retrieval produced the same byte length and substantive content for
each object. Its only differences were randomized Cloudflare email-protection
attributes on the inquiry address; the distinct repeat hashes are recorded
rather than misreported as byte identity. No challenge page is retained.

The three notices are one NIH policy lineage. The AI notice applies the GDS/
Data Use Certification framework; the RFI proposes later changes and does not
provide independent corroboration or a final replacement policy.

## GDS scope, force and effective date

Section II applies the GDS Policy to NIH-funded research generating large-scale
human or non-human genomic data and to subsequent research using those data.
It expressly includes GWAS, SNP arrays, genome sequence, transcriptomic,
metagenomic, epigenomic and gene-expression data across grants, contracts,
cooperative agreements and intramural support. NIH funding must support the
generation for a partly funded project to fall within this scope.

The notice says adherence becomes a special term and condition of the Notice of
Award or Contract Award and that noncompliance can lead to enforcement,
including withholding funding. Section III applies the policy to competing
applications due on or after 25 January 2015, contract proposals submitted on
or after that date, and intramural projects generating genomic data on or after
that date. It is therefore an NIH funding, contract and intramural policy—not a
statute or rule that binds every U.S. researcher.

> **Claim record — SRC-0054-C02 | source-report**
> **Claim:** NIH GDS is an enforceable award/contract/intramural policy for
> covered NIH-supported large-scale human and non-human genomic research and
> subsequent use, effective for the stated 25 January 2015 cohorts.
> **Claim status:** active
> **Scope:** NIH funding, contract and intramural applicability; not all U.S.
> genomic research, every NIH dataset, a general privacy law or observed
> enforcement against a named recipient.
> **As of:** Policy release 2014-08-27; package reviewed 2026-07-12.
> **Review after:** 2026-08-12.
> **Evidence status:** single-source
> **Confidence:** high

> immediately before Section III.
> **Basis / limits:** Scope, award/contract force and dates are direct. Whether
> a particular project crosses the scale/funding tests is fact-specific.

## Consent, access, exceptions and scale boundaries

The policy expects consent for future research and broad sharing for covered
post-effective-date human data and requires institutional review/certification,
data-use limitations and controlled-access terms. Section V places controlled-
access requests through NIH Data Access Committees and makes approved users and
institutions subject to the Data Use Certification and user code.

The policy also preserves bounded exceptions. Smaller studies—for example,
fewer than 100 human whole genomes—are generally outside the policy, although
an NIH Institute or Center can apply it case by case and other policies or
programme conditions may govern. For post-effective-date clinical specimens or
cell lines lacking consent, the funding Institute or Center can accept a
compelling-scientific-reason justification. Pre-effective-date specimens
receive a distinct consent review boundary. Section IV.C.6 allows a funding
Institute or Center to grant a data-submission exception where Institutional
Certification criteria cannot be met, with study registration and an alternate
sharing plan where available.

## Generative AI and controlled human genomic data

`NOT-OD-25-081` reminds approved users that GDS and the Data Use Certification
prohibit transfer of controlled-access genomic/associated data or derivatives
to an unapproved person or entity. It says providing controlled-access data to
public third-party generative-AI tools through prompts or interfaces violates
non-transferability. NIH treats generative-AI models, including parameters,
developed by approved users from controlled-access data as Data Derivatives.

Until future guidance, approved users may continue development only where NIH
has approved the use; they may share the model/parameters only with
collaborators who are also approved users and may not retain the model/
parameters at project closeout under the DUC termination/destruction term.
This notice is bounded to NIH controlled-access human genomic data, its
associated data, approved users/developers and DUC relationships. It is not a
general ban on AI training, all biomedical data or all U.S. researchers.

> **Claim record — SRC-0054-C04 | source-report**
> **Claim:** NIH applies controlled-data non-transferability to public
> generative-AI interfaces and treats models/parameters trained by approved
> users on controlled-access human genomic data as derivatives subject to
> approval, sharing and closeout limits pending future guidance.
> **Claim status:** active
> **Scope:** NIH controlled-access human genomic/associated data and DUC-bound
> users; not public data, every AI model, a criminal prohibition or a general
> U.S. AI/research rule.
> **As of:** Notice released 2025-03-28; reviewed 2026-07-12.

## Later proposal is not a final replacement

`NOT-OD-26-023` is titled a Request for Information on a *Draft* NIH
Controlled-Access Data Policy and *Proposed* GDS revisions. It was released on
17 December 2025, requested voluntary comments by 18 March 2026 and repeatedly
uses proposal language. Its draft would broaden controlled-access treatment
for NIH-supported human data and change GDS scope, thresholds, consent and
sharing practices, but the notice supplies no final issuance or effective date.

The RFI therefore cannot be used as an operative policy or to overwrite the
2014 scope. The retained official package and bounded currentness search found
no final successor; that search result is dated and does not establish that no
later action can exist after review.

covered award/contract conditions and the DUC relationship for approved data
