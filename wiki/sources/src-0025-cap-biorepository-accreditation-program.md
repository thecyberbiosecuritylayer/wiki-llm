## Programme identity, modality and scope

> **Claim record — SRC-0025-C02 | source-report**
> **Claim:** CAP presents BAP as a live private accreditation programme
> established in 2012 for research biorepositories and explicitly states that
> participation is voluntary.
> **Claim status:** active
> **Scope:** CAP programme identity/modality; not U.S. law, government mandate,
> independent quality ranking or proof that an organization is accredited.
> **As of:** 2026-07-12.
> **Review after:** 2026-10-10.
> **Evidence status:** single-source
> **Confidence:** high
> **Evidence:** Current programme page, opening programme description and
> `Qualifications` section; public Standards, Preamble, physical p. 1.
> **Basis / limits:** Issuer, establishment and voluntary status are direct.
> Marketing superlatives are not adopted as comparative findings.

> **Claim record — SRC-0025-C03 | source-report**
> **Claim:** BAP covers a research biorepository's physical location and
> personnel-performed consent, collection, processing, storage, QC/QA, data
> management, release and tracking activities, but excludes tissues stored for
> transplant and retained clinical samples not intended for research testing.
> **Claim status:** active
> **Scope:** Current CAP eligibility/scope/exceptions; not case-specific
> eligibility, transplant-laboratory governance or every linked programme.
> **As of:** 2026-07-12.
> **Review after:** 2026-10-10.
> **Evidence status:** single-source
> **Confidence:** high
> **Evidence:** Current programme page, `Qualifications`; public Standards,
> Preamble, physical p. 1.
> **Basis / limits:** Included activities and two exclusions are literal.
> CAP's separate Laboratory Accreditation Program is not merged into BAP.

are retained. Private checklists, facility identities, inspection findings,
specimen records, locations, storage settings and operational procedures are
not reproduced.
