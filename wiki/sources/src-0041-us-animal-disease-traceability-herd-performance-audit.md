# U.S. animal-disease traceability herd, performance and independent-audit package

## Package identity and complete review

The retained package separates three evidence roles:

1. the official GPO 2025 edition of `9 CFR Part 86`, checked against a dated
   eCFR XML snapshot requested for 2026-07-09;
2. the four-page APHIS issuer report on Traceability Performance Measure
   exercises for 2013–2022; and
3. the 37-page USDA OIG performance audit `33601-0003-41` plus both current
   recommendations-result pages.

All ten CFR pages, four performance-report pages, 37 audit pages, the complete
XML and both retained OIG HTML pages were reviewed. The six declared objects
total 2,589,147 bytes and hash-match the frontmatter. The current OIG-linked
PDF retrieved on 2026-07-12 was byte-identical to the retained report.

## Literal herd and adjacent asset classes

`9 CFR §86.1` directly defines a commuter herd as a herd of cattle or bison
moved between premises under normal management and a commuter-herd agreement
between herd owners and State/Tribal animal-health officials. It separately
defines flock and group/lot classes. Those adjacent classes are not synonyms.

The same section supplies herd-unique location/premises-based identifiers,
individual and group identifiers, official documents, owners, shippers,
accredited veterinarians and Federal/State/Tribal authorities. Sections
`86.3–86.5` add recordkeeping, official-identification and movement-document
functions; the current text requires specified cattle/bison tags applied after
2024-11-05 to be visually and electronically readable.

> **Claim record — SRC-0041-C03 | source-report**
> **Claim:** Current U.S. `9 CFR Part 86` directly represents a literal cattle/
> bison herd, herd owners, animal-health authorities and herd-unique records,
> alongside explicitly separate flock, group/lot, individual, premises,
> document, device, veterinarian and movement classes.
> **Claim status:** active
> **Scope:** Federal interstate animal-disease-traceability classes; not every
> herd, species, intrastate movement, system deployment or health outcome.
> **As of:** Dated eCFR snapshot 2026-07-09.
> **Review after:** 2026-10-10.
> **Evidence status:** single-source
> **Confidence:** high
> **Evidence:** GPO PDF pp. 2–5 (printed pp. 410–413), `§86.1`, `§86.3` and
> `§86.4`; dated eCFR XML sections 86.1, 86.3 and 86.4.
> **Basis / limits:** The terms, actors and records are direct. Herd, flock,
> group/lot and premises are preserved as non-equivalent units.

> **Claim record — SRC-0041-C04 | source-report**

> **Evidence status:** single-source

## Deployed program and Traceability Performance Measure exercises

APHIS reports that the ADT program began in 2013 and uses four uniformly
measured traceback/trace-forward exercise questions. The recorded elapsed time
runs from cooperator notification of an official identifier to the requested
answer and can depend on electronic/paper records or third-party contacts.

The issuer report states that combined average elapsed time for TPM 2–4 fell
from a 490-hour pre-2013 baseline to 11.5 hours in year six. In years seven and
eight, each reported national average and median for TPM 1–4 was approximately
one hour or less; year-eight averages were `0.67/0.57/0.83/0.65` hours and
medians `0.30/0.28/0.35/0.34` hours.

> **Claim record — SRC-0041-C05 | source-report**
> **Claim:** APHIS documents an implemented, annually exercised animal-disease
> traceability program with a pre-program baseline, defined elapsed-time
> measure and multi-year national cooperator results.
> **Claim status:** active
> **Scope:** Covered cattle/bison identification-record retrieval exercises;
> not disease prevention, universal livestock coverage, causal attribution to
> one safeguard, cyber-incident response or independently reproduced metrics.
> **As of:** 2013–2022 cooperative-agreement periods.
> **Review after:** 2027-01-12.
> **Evidence status:** single-source
> **Confidence:** high
> **Evidence:** APHIS performance PDF pp. 1–4, especially Background, TPM
> definitions, Performance Period Comparisons, Figure 1 and Table 1.
> **Basis / limits:** Deployment, exercise and values are direct issuer
> reporting. The report is not an independent evaluation.

> **Claim record — SRC-0041-C06 | source-report**
> **Claim:** The APHIS metrics have material design limits: coverage is a
> subset of cattle/bison, some traces terminate for missing records/data,
> Tribal exemptions apply, and the 2020–2022 method used fewer scheduled,
> advance-notice traces that could be prioritized as real disease work.
> **Claim status:** active
> **Scope:** Metric interpretation and comparability limits; not evidence that
> reported times are false or that the program has no value.
> **As of:** 2013–2022 report.
> **Review after:** 2027-01-12.
> **Evidence status:** single-source
> **Confidence:** high
> **Evidence:** APHIS performance PDF pp. 1–4, especially pp. 2–4.
> **Basis / limits:** The issuer states these coverage/method qualifications.

## Independent OIG assessment and failure evidence

USDA OIG is organizationally separate from APHIS's program/issuer role. Its
nationwide performance audit covered FY2017–2020 fieldwork across three
non-statistically selected States and the universe of 11 represented cattle-
disease incidents in those States. OIG reviewed trace-investigation records,
not merely programme descriptions.

OIG found that APHIS had not adequately managed identified traceability risks.
One of the 11 reviewed incidents was not successfully traced back due to a
known risk. The audit also notes that APHIS exercises had improved but covered
officially identified cattle and were not designed to assess the four gaps.

> **Claim record — SRC-0041-C07 | source-report**
> **Claim:** An organizationally independent USDA OIG performance audit
> reviewed trace documentation for 11 cattle-disease incidents across three
> sampled States and found one unsuccessful traceback associated with a known
> program risk.
> **Claim status:** active
> **Scope:** Non-statistical FY2017–2020 sample for tuberculosis, brucellosis
> and bovine spongiform encephalopathy response; not a national failure rate,
> unique public incident inventory or cyberattack corpus.
> **As of:** Audit fieldwork 2020–2022; report 2022-11-28.
> **Review after:** 2027-01-12.
> **Evidence status:** single-source
> **Confidence:** high
> **Evidence:** OIG PDF pp. 1–5 and 20–22; cover summary, Finding 1 footnote 9,
> Scope and Methodology, footnotes 68–71.
> **Basis / limits:** Audit scope, sample and finding are direct. OIG warns the
> selection is non-statistical and does not establish prevalence.

> **Claim record — SRC-0041-C08 | source-report**
> **Claim:** OIG independently evaluates APHIS-identified coverage and
> identification/correlation gaps, finds risk-analysis deficiencies and
> concludes that delayed tracing can conditionally increase disease-spread or
> business-disruption risk, while making no representation about the adequacy
> of agency computer systems.
> **Claim status:** active
> **Scope:** Audited program oversight and traceability-risk implications; not
> proof that a specific disease spread, a system was compromised, or every
> recommendation produced an effective outcome.
> **As of:** Report 2022-11-28.
> **Review after:** 2027-01-12.
> **Evidence status:** single-source
> **Confidence:** high
> **Evidence:** OIG PDF pp. 3–7 and 20–22, Finding 1 and Scope/Methodology.
> **Basis / limits:** Findings and conditional effects are direct. The audit
> explicitly disclaims an independent agency-computer-system assessment.
