# HHS Grants Policy Statement v2 synthetic-nucleic-acid procurement award condition, current 2025–2026

## Bibliographic identity

The U.S. Department of Health and Human Services (`HHS`) identifies the *HHS
Grants Policy Statement* (`GPS`) as Version 2.0, effective 1 October 2025. It
replaces the April 2025 GPS. A technical edit published 18 December 2025 added
links to 2 CFR 300 and corrected language in section 2.3.4.8; HHS gives that
edit the same 1 October 2025 effective date. The retained text does not state a
separate original publication date for Version 2.0, so none is inferred.

This source note focuses on the GPS-wide award-term, applicability, flow-down
and enforcement rules and Appendix D section `D.5.2.3`, “Research Involving
Potential Procurement of Synthetic DNA and RNA.” It does not interpret every
HHS award programme or substitute for a specific Notice of Award (`NoA`).

## Award-term character and enforcement frame

Chapter 1 says the GPS is incorporated by reference in the official NoA as a
standard term and condition and will be used for enforcement. Section 1.4 says
the NoA contains the terms and conditions of the specific award. Section 2.6
calls the NoA a legal instrument and says its contents become binding once
accepted. Chapter 3 describes monitoring and remedies for noncompliance; those
general remedies do not prove that any nucleic-acid condition was violated or
enforced in a particular award.

## Applicability, NIH exclusion and flow-down

Version 2.0 applies to awards and award modifications adding funding made on
or after 1 October 2025, including supplements and competing or non-competing
continuations. HHS says it applies to discretionary recipients except NIH and
flows down to subrecipients. NIH awards use the separate NIH GPS. Other than
Appendix D, this HHS GPS generally does not apply to non-discretionary awards,
and it does not apply to awards to individuals; agencies retain the stated
discretion to apply selected provisions in those settings.

Section 3.5.1 separately says applicable award terms flow to subawards and
subrecipients unless a policy or award term says otherwise and requires written
subaward agreements to carry applicable GPS requirements. This establishes a
policy flow-down design, not proof that every agreement contains a correct
clause.

## Synthetic-nucleic-acid procurement condition

Appendix D `D.5.2.3` says that, beginning 26 April 2025, scientific research
awards with potential procurement of synthetic nucleic acids or benchtop
nucleic-acid synthesis equipment need a condition requiring recipients to
adhere to the 2024 OSTP screening framework and use HHS funds to procure the
covered materials/equipment from sources adhering to it. HHS states that the
framework requirement applies to all work performed under the award.

Only that high-level funding/procurement predicate is retained here. Detailed
biological scope examples, sequence thresholds and screening mechanics are
not reproduced because they are unnecessary to establish the governance
condition and could add dual-use operational detail.

> **Claim record — SRC-0060-C05 | source-report**
> **Claim:** HHS GPS `D.5.2.3` requires covered scientific-research awards to
> include a condition under which recipients adhere to the OSTP screening
> framework and use HHS funds to procure covered synthetic-nucleic-acid
> materials or equipment from adhering sources; HHS says the framework
> requirement applies to all work performed under the award.
> **Claim status:** active
> **Scope:** Award-condition and procurement-source rule stated in the current

## Framework-currency tension

The current HHS text still attributes `D.5.2.3` to EO 14110 and points to the
2024 OSTP framework. The separately retained EO 14292 later directed OSTP to
revise or replace that framework and directed funding agencies to use providers
or manufacturers adhering to the updated framework. Neither the HHS GPS nor EO
14292 identifies the completed successor in the present corpus.

This is a currentness and reconciliation issue, not permission to silently call
the 2024 text fully current, rescinded, replaced or equivalent to an unknown
successor. The HHS GPS proves a current HHS award-policy predicate; it does not
resolve the status or content of the ordered OSTP successor.

## Scope, methods and limitations

This is an official agency grants-policy instrument, not an empirical study.
The represented unit is an HHS award-policy relationship: agency/NoA,
recipient, possible subrecipient and procurement from an adhering source. It
does not report a provider population, award sample, procurement transaction,
conformity method, audit, enforcement action, screening benchmark, biological
outcome or causal risk reduction.

Both retained objects are normalized text from one HHS lineage. Their
agreement supports faithful extraction but not independent evidence. The
normalization preserves readable structure and links but not upstream PDF/HTML
bytes, visual layout or cryptographic provenance. The claims use section and
hash-bound line locators so the retained text remains reproducible.

## Safety handling

The note retains only actors, dates, award scope, procurement-source condition,
flow-down and evidence limits. It omits exact biological examples, sequence
thresholds, screening logic, concern criteria, reporting contacts and any
evasion or equipment-bypass detail.
