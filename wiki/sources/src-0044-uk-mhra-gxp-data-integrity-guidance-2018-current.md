# UK MHRA GxP data-integrity guidance, 2018 with current-status wrapper

## Identity, acquisition and complete review

The retained package contains the complete 76,369-byte GOV.UK presentation
and the complete 21-page, 456,031-byte MHRA Revision 1 guidance. The two
declared objects total 532,400 bytes and match the frontmatter hashes. Every
PDF page and the landing page's identity, details, update history and GLP note
were reviewed.

The PDF is tagged, unencrypted and unsigned, with no form, JavaScript or
embedded-file component detected. Its generic metadata title/subject `letter`
is an artifact defect; the cover, footer and official wrapper provide the
actual identity. Text extraction was complete. `qpdf` was unavailable and the
local signature trust database failed to initialize before the tool reported
no file signatures.

An official repeat PDF retrieval was byte-identical on 2026-07-12. The repeat
GOV.UK wrapper had a different hash but retained the same title, 2018
publication, 2021 update record, substantive details and PDF. The dynamic
wrapper is not a second source or stable-byte content component.

## Data-governance obligations and risk model

The guidance requires organizational responsibility and a documented state
of control based on data-integrity risk. It addresses paper, electronic and
hybrid records; unauthorized deletion/amendment/exclusion; configurable
systems; data transfer; human intervention; audit/review; CAPA and regulator
notification.

> **Claim record — SRC-0044-C04 | source-report**
> **Claim:** MHRA sets a risk-based data-governance model covering data
> criticality/lifecycle, documented controls and rationale, DIRA, periodic
> audit, CAPA and notification of significant data-integrity incidents.
> **Claim status:** active
> **Scope:** Regulatory guidance expectations; not evidence of a specific
> facility's compliance, detection performance or successful remediation.
> **As of:** Revision 1 March 2018.
> **Review after:** 2026-10-10.
> **Evidence status:** single-source
> **Confidence:** high
> **Evidence:** PDF pp. 3-6, §§2.5-4.6; especially §§3.4-3.9 and 4.1-4.6.
> **Basis / limits:** Expected activities and risk factors are direct; results
> are not reported.

> **Claim record — SRC-0044-C05 | source-report**
> **Claim:** The guidance identifies manipulation opportunity across
> configurable electronic systems, system-to-system transfer and human
> intervention, and states that automation or validated status may lower but
> does not eliminate data-integrity risk.
> **Claim status:** active
> **Scope:** General GxP vulnerability/risk mechanisms; not a cyber incident,

> **As of:** Revision 1 March 2018.
> **Review after:** 2026-10-10.

## Exceptions, adoption and lineage boundaries

The source excludes medical devices. It says routine forensic checking is not
expected, permits risk-proportionate effort and documents bounded alternatives
for limited-login systems. The 2021 wrapper says OECD's 2021 GLP advisory takes
precedence for UK GLP under Mutual Acceptance of Data while MHRA remains
supplementary. The wrapper also says inspection failures have led to regulatory
action; it does not quantify organization-level implementation.
