## Scope, terminology and modality

The guide defines a biospecimen resource as human specimens and associated data
for research, the physical structure in which the collection is stored, and
all associated processes/policies. It says the term broadly covers biobanks and
biorepositories and applies from formal organizations to a researcher's freezer.
Adoption is voluntary and adaptable. It does not provide detailed laboratory
procedures; post-mortem recovery has a separate guide.

> **Claim record — SRC-0022-C03 | source-report**
> **Claim:** NCI defines a human-research biospecimen resource/biobank/
> biorepository as material plus associated data, storage structure and
> processes/policies, and presents its Fourth Edition as voluntary adaptable
> best-practices guidance rather than detailed procedure or binding standard.
> **Claim status:** active
> **Scope:** Human research biospecimen resources; not diagnostic-laboratory,
> high-containment, non-human repository or accreditation completeness.
> **As of:** 2026-01.
> **Review after:** 2027-01-08.

## Governance, consent and material/data custody

Governance begins before recruitment/collection and follows consent, permitted
use, access, distribution and ultimate disposition. Participant choices and
withdrawal must be tracked; already distributed/generated data create explicit
limits. Material/data access, custodianship/stewardship and transfer agreements
remain distinct responsibility boundaries.

> **Claim record — SRC-0022-C05 | source-report**
> **Claim:** NCI connects consent/participant choices and governance to
> collection, access/use, material/data sharing, custody/stewardship, withdrawal
> and final disposition, with explicit limits once data/material have left the
> resource.

## Physical material lifecycle and closeout

The guide covers planning, collection/receipt, identification, processing,
inventory/storage, retrieval, use/dissemination, transfer/shipping/receiving,
and final transfer/disposal. `Accession` is not its preferred term: the frozen
rubric stage must be represented only as receipt/acquisition plus assignment of
a unique identifier and inventory entry. Legacy plans cover loss of leadership/
funding, completion, unknown provenance, transfer/reassignment and ethical
disposal of both specimens and associated data.

## Informatics, identity and auditability

Section C.6 recommends validated systems that track acquisition, processing,
handling, quality, inventory and associated data. Unique physical/digital IDs,
derivative lineage, consent/data associations, real-time location, RBAC,
project permissions, identity-bearing access logs and tamper-resistant change
audit trails support material–data custody without exposing participant identity.
