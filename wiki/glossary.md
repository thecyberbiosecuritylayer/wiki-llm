# Glossary

This glossary records concise canonical formulations and links to full concept
pages. Definitions are added after the relevant normative or primary sources
have been ingested. Source-language terms remain as aliases when translation
could introduce ambiguity.

## B

### Biological risk management

WHO 2024 uses this term as an umbrella or continuum encompassing biosafety and
laboratory biosecurity. This is a source-specific laboratory framing, not a
universal label for all health, environmental, or agricultural risk programs.
Source: [SRC-0004](sources/src-0004-who-laboratory-biosecurity-guidance-2024.md),
Glossary, printed p. xiii; §1 and Figure 1.1.

### Biosafety

In WHO 2024, the containment principles, technologies, and practices intended
to prevent unintentional exposure to biological agents and their inadvertent
release. This is a direct WHO laboratory definition; jurisdictional and
sectoral usage is tracked in
[QUE-0001](questions/que-0001-domain-boundaries.md). Source:
[SRC-0004](sources/src-0004-who-laboratory-biosecurity-guidance-2024.md),
Glossary, printed p. xiii.

### Biosecurity (laboratory)

In WHO 2024, the protection, control, and accountability of biological
material, technology, and information, and related equipment, methods, skills,
and data, to prevent unauthorized access, loss, theft, misuse, diversion,
release, or weaponization. The definition includes both intentional and
accidental unauthorized access, so it does not create an absolute boundary
with biosafety. Source:
[SRC-0004](sources/src-0004-who-laboratory-biosecurity-guidance-2024.md),
Glossary, printed p. xiii.

## C

### Community Profile (NIST Framework context)

In NIST IR 8467 2pd, a baseline of prioritized CSF/PF outcomes for a community
that an organization should adapt to its own obligations, environment, and
Mission Objectives and use for Current/Target Profile comparison. This is a
source-specific **draft** profile, not a universal control baseline,
certification, or implementation result. Source:
[SRC-0006](sources/src-0006-nist-ir-8467-genomic-data-profile-2024.md),
§§3.1–3.3, printed pp. 12–19 (PDF pp. 23–30).

### Cyberbiosecurity

**Aliases:** cyberbiosecurity; cyber-biosecurity.

The operational field for analyzing coupled risk in which digital and
biological systems are connected by a dependency or a consequence-transfer
mechanism, so that a state change in one may affect the other. This is the
wiki's editorial definition, not a claim of universal consensus. Full page:
[CON-0001](concepts/con-0001-cyberbiosecurity.md). Normative boundaries between
adjacent terms and source-specific overlap are tracked in
[QUE-0001](questions/que-0001-domain-boundaries.md).

### Cybersecurity (laboratory context)

WHO 2024 associates the availability, integrity, authentication, and
confidentiality of digital systems and information with their protection and
restoration, and explicitly includes laboratory equipment and building systems
within laboratory cyber scope. This does not assert that any particular
facility is exposed to a network. Source:
[SRC-0004](sources/src-0004-who-laboratory-biosecurity-guidance-2024.md),
Glossary, printed p. xiv.

## G

### Genomic data

In NIST IR 8432/8467, data generated by studying the structure and function of
a genome; examples include DNA sequences, variants, modifications, gene
activity, and annotations. The term may apply to human, microbiome, microbial,
model-organism, animal, or plant contexts, so `genomic data` should not
automatically be equated solely with human genetic information. Full page:
[AST-0001](assets/ast-0001-genomic-data.md). Sources:
[SRC-0005](sources/src-0005-nist-ir-8432-cybersecurity-genomic-data-2023.md),
§§1–2, printed pp. 1, 3 (PDF pp. 10, 12);
[SRC-0006](sources/src-0006-nist-ir-8467-genomic-data-profile-2024.md),
§1.2, printed pp. 4–7 (PDF pp. 15–18).

## L

### LINDDUN

Privacy threat-modeling taxonomy used by NIST SP 1800-43C for each dataflow
segment, covering Linking, Identifying, Non-repudiation, Detecting, Data
Disclosure, Unawareness/Unintervenability, and Non-compliance. A taxonomy match
is not evidence that a threat occurred or is likely. Volume A misspells the
name `LINDUNN`, and Volume C Appendix A omits Non-repudiation from its acronym
expansion; Appendix C supplies the complete list. Source:
[SRC-0007](sources/src-0007-nist-sp-1800-43-genomic-privacy-threat-modeling-2025.md),
Volume C §2.2.1, printed pp. 20–24 (PDF pp. 29–33); static Appendix C.

## P

### PANOPTIC

Pattern and Action Nomenclature Of Privacy Threats In Context: a taxonomy of
contextual domains and privacy activities used in SP 1800-43C to construct
possible privacy-attack scenarios and cross-check LINDDUN entries. In this
wiki, it is an analyzer of possibilities, not an observed-attack corpus or an
empirical validation. Source:
[SRC-0007](sources/src-0007-nist-sp-1800-43-genomic-privacy-threat-modeling-2025.md),
Volume C §§1.6 and 2.2.2–2.2.3, printed pp. 6 and 24–30
(PDF pp. 15 and 33–39); static Appendix C.

### Privacy Risk Assessment Methodology (PRAM)

A NIST method with worksheets for framing objectives and governance, assessing
system design, prioritizing risk, and selecting controls. SP 1800-43C adapts
the first two worksheets to document context, privacy capabilities, and
dataflows for a threat-modeling exercise; that application is not a completed
organizational privacy risk assessment. Source:
[SRC-0007](sources/src-0007-nist-sp-1800-43-genomic-privacy-threat-modeling-2025.md),
Volume C §§1.5–1.6 and 2.1, printed pp. 5–12 (PDF pp. 14–21); static
Appendix C.

### Privacy risk in genomic-data processing

In the NIST IR 8432/8467 framing, privacy problems may arise either from a
cyberattack or without one, through inadequate predictability, manageability,
or disassociability during processing. Privacy therefore cannot be reduced to
confidentiality. Sources:
[SRC-0005](sources/src-0005-nist-ir-8432-cybersecurity-genomic-data-2023.md),
§1.1 and footnote 1, printed pp. 1–2 (PDF pp. 10–11);
[SRC-0006](sources/src-0006-nist-ir-8467-genomic-data-profile-2024.md),
§§2.2–2.2.1 and Figures 2–3, printed pp. 9–11 (PDF pp. 20–22).

### Problematic data action

In the Privacy Framework terminology reproduced in IR 8467, a data action that
could cause an adverse effect for individuals. It is not necessarily a
cyberattack or unauthorized access. This wiki uses the term in
[RSK-0005](risk-scenarios/rsk-0005-permitted-genomic-processing-privacy-harm.md)
only at a high level, without re-identification instructions. Source:
[SRC-0006](sources/src-0006-nist-ir-8467-genomic-data-profile-2024.md),
Appendix C, printed p. 174 (PDF p. 185); §2.2 and Figure 3.

## T

### Threat-model validation (SP 1800-43C)

An internal cross-check in which a possible PANOPTIC attack must correspond to
a LINDDUN entry and threaten at least one privacy engineering objective. This
is consistency `validation` of the example model, not empirical attack
validation, likelihood estimation, a completeness test, or a control-
effectiveness result. Source:
[SRC-0007](sources/src-0007-nist-sp-1800-43-genomic-privacy-threat-modeling-2025.md),
Volume C §2.2.3 and Table 15, printed pp. 28–30 (PDF pp. 37–39); static
Appendices D and G.
