# UK–US national One Health governance reconciliation

## Exact question and method

This synthesis tests only frozen criterion `AGE-GR`: current animal/plant-
health, environmental and cyber/data instruments from at least two
jurisdictions must be reconciled under One Health at SF2. It does not test
technical integration, national compliance, complete agriculture governance,
implementation effectiveness or the other nine AGE cells.

The evidence cutoff is 2026-07-12. Independence rests on two national official
lineages with different issuers and jurisdictions: the UK Cabinet Office BSS
and the U.S. OHCU/CDC/DOI/USDA NOHF-Zoonoses. UK strategy, UK implementation
report and UKDI announcement remain one UK government policy/current-use
lineage for this claim; the three U.S. lead agencies jointly issue one U.S.
interagency framework lineage. Neither side is multiplied by document count or
agency count.

The grammar is collective: the reconciled two-jurisdiction portfolio must cover
all named instrument classes, but every class need not be duplicated in each
jurisdiction. Both jurisdictions must nonetheless supply a direct, current
national One Health instrument and their scope, modality, applicability,
implementation and missing predicates must be compared explicitly.

> **Claim record — SYN-0019-C01 | analytic-judgment**
> **Claim:** UK BSS and U.S. NOHF-Zoonoses are materially independent current
> national One Health policy lineages, and their multiple same-jurisdiction/
> same-policy-lineage or joint-agency records are not counted as additional jurisdictions or
> independent effectiveness evidence.
> **Claim status:** active
> **Scope:** SF2 provenance and counting for `AGE-GR`; not claim-level
> independence for a shared technical control, event, implementation result or
> measured effect.
> **As of:** 2026-07-12.
> **Review after:** 2026-07-26.

## Literal instrument-portfolio reconciliation

| Frozen limb | United Kingdom BSS | United States NOHF-Zoonoses | Accepted reconciliation and limit |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| One Health frame | explicit interconnected human, animal, plant and environmental/ecosystem health plus cross-government/cross-disciplinary collaboration | explicit people, domestic/wild animals, plants and shared terrestrial/aquatic environments | both are direct national One Health frames, not editorially joined sector documents |
| Animal health | notifiable/zoonotic animal disease, livestock response/surveillance and animal-health bodies are direct | domestic/wild animals, animal health, zoonoses, surveillance and outbreak response are direct | animal-health instruments/functions are direct in two jurisdictions; no common disease list or outcome is inferred |
| Plant health | plant pests/disease, Plant Health Risk Group/Register, surveillance, data-system/contingency and border functions are direct, with mixed current/future maturity | plants are in the One Health definition and broader issue list, while the operative focus is zoonoses/public-health and no full plant-production regime is supplied | plant-health instrument depth is asymmetric; UK supplies the direct substantive limb and U.S. plants preserve the national One Health boundary |
| Environment/ecosystems | environment, invasive species, terrestrial/freshwater/marine monitoring and national biosurveillance ambitions are direct | shared terrestrial/aquatic environments, ecosystems and multi-domain surveillance/model inputs are direct | environmental governance is direct in both; no shared monitoring architecture or validated network is inferred |
| Data instruments | standards/sharing protocols, Radar, biosurveillance, plant/environment data and biodata-risk work are direct; full technical integration is not demonstrated | secure information/sample sharing, interoperability, surveillance/model and laboratory reporting are direct objectives; deployment is not demonstrated | two national data-governance designs are reconciled without claiming interoperability between them |
| Cyber instrument | cyberbiosecurity risk from bioscience/robotics/ML/AI convergence and biodata-security/responsible-innovation priorities coexist in the UK portfolio; no tested baseline/path/effect | NOHF calls for secure sharing and laboratory biosecurity but has no explicit cyberbio control regime in this artifact | collective cyber/data limb is satisfied by explicit UK cyberbio plus both data portfolios; cyber maturity is not duplicated or treated as implementation |

The UK's all-threat/national-security frame includes natural, accidental and
deliberate risks and comparatively broad plant/environment/cyberbio content.
The U.S. framework uses a broad One Health definition but states that its
programme primarily focuses on zoonoses and public-health preparedness and
does not specifically address every other One Health issue. This is a material
scope difference, not a defect to normalize away.

## Safety boundary

This synthesis contains only public national policy classes, actor types and
evidence-state comparisons. It omits sample/pathogen detail, facility/farm
topology, credentials, assay/screening parameters, exploit mechanics and
target-specific weaknesses.
