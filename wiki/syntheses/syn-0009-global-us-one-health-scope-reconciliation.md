# Global and US One Health agriculture/environment scope reconciliation

## Question and method

This synthesis tests the exact frozen `AGE-ST` contract: One Health boundaries
must cover crops, livestock, aquaculture/wildlife and environmental monitoring,
with exclusions, at SF2. It does not ask whether any programme is implemented
or whether the rest of the agriculture dimension is complete.

Cutoff is 2026-07-12. Three source notes are used, but independence rests on two
materially distinct substantive lineages: the U.S. CDC/DOI/USDA NOHF and the
global WHO/FAO/UNEP/WOAH guide. The four Quadripartite issuers are one joint
lineage, not four. The independent Drape academic/workshop study adds literal
crop/livestock terminology and a cyberbiosecurity boundary but is not needed to
manufacture global-programme independence.

## Provenance and normalization rules

- `plants + agriculture` is normalized only to the crop/plant-health scope
  limb; it is not a crop inventory or production lifecycle;
- `domestic animals + livestock` is normalized only to the livestock limb;
- `aquatic animals + aquaculture` and `wild animals + wildlife` retain their
  distinct words and are not inferred from generic animal health;
- environmental monitoring requires direct monitoring language, not merely a
  statement that ecosystems matter;
- a stated programme focus or non-technical-area limit is an exclusion/boundary,
  not evidence that omitted systems do not exist;
- programme guidance, scope and desired impact are never implementation,
  incident, test or effectiveness evidence.

## Safety boundary

This reconciliation contains public sector/scope classes and programme
functions only. It omits farm/facility topology, species/pathogen inventories,
sampling details, access criteria, control settings and actionable misuse paths.
