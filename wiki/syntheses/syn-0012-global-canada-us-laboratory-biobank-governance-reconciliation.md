# Global, Canadian and U.S. laboratory-biobank governance reconciliation

## Question, cutoff and lineage accounting

This synthesis tests only the frozen `LAB-GR` criterion after complete
`SRC-0025` integration. Cutoff is 2026-07-12.

WHO, PHAC/CFIA Canada and CAP are separate issuer/development lineages. ISO is
one standard-family lineage; its published 2018 edition, FDIS successor and
ISO 15189 comparator do not create three independent sources. NCI and CAP are
also not double-counted for NCI/ISO-derived checklist substance: CAP is
independent only for its own voluntary programme, inspection, exclusion and
issuer-reported accreditation/adoption predicates.

> **Claim record — SYN-0012-C01 | analytic-judgment**
> **Claim:** The portfolio is SF2-appropriate for governance reconciliation:
> independent WHO global guidance, Canadian national-standard/licence context
> and CAP private accreditation-programme evidence are supplemented by bounded
> NCI and ISO biobank instruments without double-counting derivative content.
> **Claim status:** active
> **Scope:** Independence/modality for `LAB-GR`; not independent accreditation
> effectiveness, legal equivalence or cross-compliance.
> **As of:** 2026-07-12.
> **Review after:** 2026-07-26.
> **Evidence status:** corroborated

## Force, adoption and exception matrix

| Instrument/context | Laboratory/biobank scope | Force and adoption state | Exceptions/conflicts/gaps |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| WHO 2024 global laboratory biosecurity | diagnostic, research, mobile, manufacturing/pharmaceutical and related laboratory settings | nonbinding normative guidance; country/institution adoption not reported by the source | risk/context tailoring; not a biobank accreditation scheme or universal law |
| Canadian Biosafety Standard Third Edition | regulated facilities/material/activity under HPTA/HAR and HAAHPR contexts | national standard below enabling law but mandatory through applicable licence/permit conditions | defined material/activity exemptions; aquatic/plant regimes separate; facility compliance/results unknown |
| NCI Best Practices Fourth Edition | voluntary U.S. human-research biospecimen resources/biobanks | official voluntary adaptable guidance; adoption rate unknown | not diagnostic/HCL/accreditation law; separate detailed/current accreditation needed |
| ISO 20387:2018 | international research/development biobanking competence/operation standard | published Edition 1; legal incorporation/accreditation adoption not established by ISO metadata | food/feed and therapeutic exclusions; diagnostic/treatment branch to ISO 15189; Edition 2 only FDIS stage 50.00 |
| CAP BAP | U.S.-based voluntary private accreditation for research biorepository location/activities with international participation | scheme conditions for participants; biennial inspection/off-year self-inspection; issuer associates the programme with over 100 biorepositories worldwide and separately provides a live accredited directory | excludes transplant tissues and retained nonresearch clinical samples; CAP PT/EQA enrollment not required; no statutory force or effectiveness inference |
