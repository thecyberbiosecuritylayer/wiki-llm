# Cross-sector control, threat-asset and governance reconciliation

## Question, corpus and counting rules

This page tests exactly `CTR-AS`, `CTR-WL`, `CTR-SD`, `GOV-WL`, `GOV-TV`,
`GOV-XT` and `THI-AS`. It uses only active claims that predate this page. It
does not manufacture deployment, effectiveness, incidents, legal equivalence
or a universal lifecycle from shared vocabulary.

The seven applied sectors are GEN, SEB, LAB, BMO, CPH, AGE and AIB. A control
counts only when its objective is connected to named assets/stakeholders and a
specific lifecycle/scenario edge. An implementation claim counts only at its
reported scope. Threat-matrix rows retain actor-known/unknown,
malicious/non-adversarial and hypothetical/controlled/observed states.

WHO laboratory, NIST genomic/OT, U.S./UK screening, ICH, WHO clinical-lab,
Drape/PATH-SAFE, NASEM and the DOJ/EU legal instruments are materially
independent issuer/method lineages. USENIX supplies controlled evidence rather
than an incident. NCI and WHO laboratory sources complement but are not
counted as legal or jurisdictional equivalents.
