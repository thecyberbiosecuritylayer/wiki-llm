# US–UK synthetic nucleic-acid procurement screening comparison

> Two independent 2024 official instruments converge on a safe
> order→screen→review→decision→record/equipment-accountability model. They do
> not have the same force, covered population or assurance design, and the
> 2024 texts themselves supply no adoption or effectiveness evidence. A 2025
> U.S. executive order adds a revise-or-replace direction; current HHS GPS now
> adds one bounded policy-implementation branch, not a verified successor,
> individual award census or effectiveness result.

## Question and scope

How do the 2024 US OSTP framework and UK DSIT guidance allocate responsibility
for synthetic nucleic-acid and benchtop-equipment procurement screening, and
which similarities can be used without treating them as legally or empirically
equivalent?

Cutoff is 2026-07-13. The comparison uses SRC-0011 and SRC-0012 for the two
2024 normative designs, SRC-0013 for the later U.S. executive direction and
SRC-0060 for one current bounded HHS award-policy mechanism. Embedded legal
references, the completed U.S. successor, other agency/award terms and UK
implementation reports remain separate evidence needs.

## Method and evidence base

Each artifact was read fully and coded on instrument type, jurisdiction, force,
population, roles, lifecycle stages, control functions, exceptions, assurance
and evidence maturity. Similar wording in the US and UK 2024 instruments is
counted as independent normative convergence because the issuers and
instruments are separate; it is not implementation corroboration or `SF3`
evidence. EO 14292 is a later US status lineage, not a third jurisdiction or a
second implementation lineage.

## Comparative findings

| Dimension | United States 2024 | United Kingdom 2024 | Reconciliation |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Instrument/force | Funding-condition design implemented through agency authority/terms; broader use encouraged | Voluntary responsible-practice guidance; separate law remains applicable | Neither is generalized into universal law |
| Population | Federally funded life-sciences procurement and relevant providers/manufacturers/intermediaries | UK-operating providers, manufacturers, principal/end users and vendors | Role names differ; both cover intermediary and equipment lifecycle |
| Core flow | accountable adherence → order/customer checks → decision/report → records/security | customer+sequence checks → follow-up → fulfill/deny/report → records/transfer/security | Shared safe lifecycle, not identical procedure |
| Equipment | legitimate acquisition/access and later transfer accountability | authentication, updated screening, logging/process integrity and transfer/use controls | Functional convergence; no device benchmark |
| Assurance | self-attestation plus planned specification/conformity/audit work | public alignment representation and guidance expectations; no central conformity result | Attestation/disclosure is not effectiveness |
| Exceptions | Rare case-specific agency exception plus separate bounded technical exception | No equivalent framework-wide agency exception in this guidance | Legal/licensing scopes are not substitutes |
| Current status | EO 14292 ordered revision/replacement; successor unverified; HHS GPS adds one bounded current policy branch excluding NIH | Current GOV.UK substantive text verified on cutoff | No current US–UK equivalence claim |
| Implementation evidence | Department policy-level mechanism only; no award/provider census | None in source | Adoption remains scope-specific; testing/effectiveness separate |

## Counterevidence and unresolved status

The 2024 US artifact derives from an earlier executive-policy context and
explicitly anticipated updates. EO 14292 now provides direct evidence of a
later revise-or-replace direction, while neither the order nor the bounded
official search supplies the completed successor. Current HHS GPS supplies one
bounded policy branch but no individual award/provider census. The UK source
remains live but does not prove adoption. Later official records may change
current-status conclusions without changing what the 2024 artifacts said.

## Implications

- `AST-0004` now has two independent official lineages for the procurement
  asset/stakeholder envelope.
- `CTL-0006` has two independent normative lineages for exact-edge identity,
  screening, review, instrument, reporting, record and security functions.
- WFL-0008 remains the procurement segment; WFL-0014/SYN-0032 now supply the
  separate cross-source complete lifecycle composition.
- SYS-0006 and the downstream RSK-0008 path remain unvalidated/hypothetical
  beyond the bounded screening-test and operational-data decision edge.
- U.S. governance still requires the completed successor, wider agency and
  award evidence, compliance and implementation outcomes as separate sources.

## Safety boundary

The comparison omits exact screening windows and logic, biological examples,
suspicious-order indicators, identity checklist fields, operational reporting
contacts and device/reagent bypass or anti-tamper mechanics. Those details are
unnecessary for governance and control-function comparison.

## Open questions

1. Which official successor fulfills EO 14292 §4(b), and which additional
   agencies/awards implement it?
2. What adoption/conformity evidence exists in either jurisdiction?
3. What independent benchmark reports both positive and null/failure results?
4. Which sources cover design→experiment→biological result→digital DBTL
   feedback and disposition safely?
