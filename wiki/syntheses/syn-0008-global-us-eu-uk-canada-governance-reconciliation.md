# Global, US, EU, UK and Canada governance modality and actor reconciliation

## Question and method

This synthesis asks whether the locally ingested primary corpus can distinguish
instrument classes/statuses and reconcile actors, force, adoption, exceptions,
conflicts and gaps across the frozen global, United States, European Union,
United Kingdom and one-additional-context governance criteria.

Cutoff is 2026-07-12. `Adoption` below distinguishes a normative legal/
licence-condition mechanism from observed institutional implementation. `Current`
means the issuing authority still presented the instrument/status on the cutoff;
it does not mean every provision applies, every organization adopted it or a
safeguard worked. `Not established` is an evidence boundary, not an empty cell.

## Canonical instrument-class and date matrix

Instrument class and lifecycle status are separate axes: a Community Profile
may be voluntary in use and simultaneously a public draft; a standard can be
mandatory through a licence condition without becoming an Act; a legislative
regulation can be in force while application is staged.

is not relabelled a legal duty, a protected interest is not relabelled an
individual right, and internal oversight is not called independent review.

## Residual gaps

- The current HPTA/HPTR, HAA/HAR and Biosecurity Addendum need separate full
  primary transactions before detailed current Canadian legal propositions.
- `LAB-GR` still needs biobank/repository and accreditation coverage plus
  adoption/exceptions reconciliation; CBS alone is insufficient.
- No adoption census, regulator/enforcement case, completed audit, measured
  safeguard result or independent effectiveness evaluation is present across
  two jurisdictions.
- Conflict-of-laws, provincial/Member-State/national derogations and sector-
  specific overlays remain scoped legal-analysis questions.
- The U.S. and UK lineages represented here address different regulated
  objects; cross-context matrix inclusion is not substantive equivalence.

## Safety boundary

The synthesis compares public instrument metadata, actor classes, scope and
governance mechanisms. It omits personal records, sensitive inventories,
facility topology, screening logic, access-bypass detail and harmful-production
information.
