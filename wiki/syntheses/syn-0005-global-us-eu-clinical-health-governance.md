# Global, US and EU clinical-health governance reconciliation

## Purpose and boundary

This synthesis asks whether the current corpus jointly covers the clinical-
laboratory, electronic-health-data, device/diagnostic and reporting instrument
classes required by `CPH-GR`. It compares a global WHO laboratory-biosecurity
guidance, U.S. FDA medical-device cybersecurity guidance and the EU EHDS
regulation. None is a complete substitute for clinical-laboratory accreditation,
general health-privacy law, medical-device regulation or public-health law.

## Scope comparison

| Dimension | WHO global laboratory guidance | FDA United States | EHDS European Union | Reconciled boundary |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Clinical laboratory | research, diagnostic, high-containment and manufacturing/pharmaceutical laboratory settings | device/diagnostic and qualifying biological/combination-product device contexts, not all laboratory operations | laboratory/diagnostic results and transfusion-medicine category within electronic health data, not laboratory accreditation | Laboratory operation, device product and electronic-result governance are distinct |
| Health data | information/cybersecurity as one laboratory-biosecurity component | device-related health information, submission/labeling/system safety; not general health-data law | personal/non-personal electronic health data for primary and secondary use | Data scope and legal object differ |
| Device/diagnostic | connected equipment and information systems at advisory programme level | premarket device and narrower cyber-device lifecycle | EHR-system/interoperability regime with bounded medical-device/IVD interactions | EHR conformity is not FDA device cybersecurity review |
| Reporting | incident/near-miss escalation, investigation and learning | submission evidence, labeling, vulnerability/incident communication and postmarket plans | clinical exchange/logging plus EHR serious-incident, surveillance and secondary-use reporting mechanisms | Reporting purpose, audience and trigger must be retained |
| Public health | laboratory biosecurity and national oversight support | patient/device safety rather than general surveillance authority | public-health secondary-use purposes and cross-border health-data infrastructure | No source alone covers the full public-health surveillance lifecycle |

## Force, dates, alternatives and exceptions

| Attribute | WHO | FDA | EHDS |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Instrument | global normative guidance | final U.S. nonbinding guidance, with separate cited scoped duties | binding EU regulation |
| Current reference | 2024 complete revision, official status checked 2026-07-12 | February 2026 Level 2 revision, checked 2026-07-12 | in force; English text/current status checked 2026-07-12 |
| Application/adoption | national/institutional adoption unknown | manufacturer/product implementation unknown; alternative approach can satisfy applicable requirements | general and subject-specific application staged 2027–2035; rollout unknown |
| Boundary | risk-based local/national tailoring | device/submission predicates, guidance versus QMSR/§524B modality | patient safety, opt-outs, national safeguards, other Union/national acts and staged dates |

## Reporting and responsibility boundaries

WHO connects laboratory/institutional/national reporting and incident learning.
FDA connects manufacturers/submitters, FDA, facilities, users, patients,
suppliers and vulnerability reporters across premarket and postmarket device
responsibilities. EHDS assigns provider, product, national-authority,
health-data-holder/user, Commission/Board and cross-border duties. A report to
one pathway does not satisfy another pathway by analogy.

> **Claim record — SYN-0005-C03 | analytic-judgment**
> **Claim:** Laboratory biosecurity reporting, device submission/vulnerability
> communication and EHDS clinical/EHR/secondary-use reporting have distinct
> triggers, recipients and accountability paths and cannot be treated as one
> notification regime.

## Evidence maturity

All three sources define governance or assurance design. None contains a
representative laboratory-adoption study, manufacturer control result, Member
State rollout census or cross-regime effectiveness evaluation. The existing
Synnovis/NHSBT event is a separate UK operational lineage, not implementation
of these three instruments.

> **Claim record — SYN-0005-C04 | analytic-judgment**
> **Claim:** Current clinical-health governance coverage is multi-lineage and
> multi-level, but implementation, audit and outcome evidence for the compared
> instruments remains unknown.

## Unresolved gaps

- Complete clinical/public-health workflow and asset maps remain incomplete.
- FDA device scope does not establish genomics or every LIS/EHR function.
- EHDS does not replace medical-device cybersecurity or laboratory standards.
- WHO guidance does not establish national legal adoption.
- Public-health surveillance law, implementation and outcome evidence need
  dedicated sources.
